David, a 50-year-old office worker in a fast-paced corporate job, arrived at the hospital after experiencing sharp chest pain, sweating, and dizziness while working on a stressful project. He had been leading a sedentary lifestyle, sitting at a desk for long hours, with a diet mostly consisting of fast food and sugary drinks due to the demands of his job. Blood tests revealed markedly elevated troponin levels (TnI: 5.8 ng/mL), indicating severe heart muscle damage and an LDL cholesterol level of 180 mg/dL. His ECG showed ST-segment elevation in the anterior leads, suggesting an acute anterior wall myocardial infarction. An echocardiogram showed a reduced ejection fraction of 35%, with akinesis of the anterior wall. A coronary angiogram revealed a 95% blockage in the left anterior descending (LAD) artery. David underwent an emergency percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. Post-procedure, he was started on a regimen of aspirin, statins, and beta-blockers and advised to reduce his work-related stress significantly. He was also enrolled in a cardiac rehabilitation program to improve his lifestyle and reduce his risk of future events.
