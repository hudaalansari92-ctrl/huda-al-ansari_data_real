A 50-year-old man with a 15-year history of ulcerative colitis, an autoimmune inflammatory bowel disease, was admitted to the hospital with symptoms of chest pain and shortness of breath. Although he had no traditional coronary artery disease (CAD) risk factors, he was a non-smoker, maintained a healthy weight, and had normal cholesterol and blood pressure levels, and his physician was aware of the increased cardiovascular risk associated with chronic inflammation from his autoimmune disease. Coronary angiography revealed significant CAD with a 70% blockage in the left anterior descending artery, requiring immediate stent placement. His cardiologist explained that the persistent inflammation associated with ulcerative colitis likely accelerated the development of atherosclerosis despite the absence of other risk factors. Post-procedure, he was advised to continue managing his ulcerative colitis closely, adopt a heart-healthy lifestyle, and attend regular cardiovascular check-ups to help reduce the risk of further complications.
