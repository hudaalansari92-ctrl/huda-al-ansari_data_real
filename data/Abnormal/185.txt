Samir, a 52-year-old accountant, had been living a predominantly sedentary lifestyle for over two decades due to his desk job, spending long hours sitting with minimal physical activity. His routine involved working at a computer, commuting by car, and spending leisure time watching television, with little to no exercise. His diet was high in processed foods, sugary snacks, and unhealthy fats, leading to significant weight gain and obesity (BMI 33). Although he had no history of smoking or diabetes, he was diagnosed with high blood pressure and borderline high cholesterol two years ago. Over the past year, he began experiencing fatigue, difficulty performing simple physical tasks like climbing stairs, chest tightness, mild discomfort after meals or stress, and occasional shortness of breath, especially in the evenings. Concerned about these symptoms, he sought medical advice. Diagnostic tests revealed elevated LDL cholesterol, low HDL cholesterol, mild insulin resistance, minor ECG irregularities under exertion, and early signs of plaque buildup in his coronary arteries. He was diagnosed with early-stage coronary artery disease (CAD) due to a sedentary lifestyle, obesity, and high cholesterol. His treatment plan focused on increasing physical activity, starting with 20-minute brisk walks and gradually progressing to 45 minutes five times per week, along with dietary modifications to reduce processed foods and increase fiber, lean proteins, and healthy fats. He was also enrolled in a weight-loss program targeting a 5-7% reduction in body weight and prescribed statins and a mild antihypertensive to lower his cardiovascular risk. Regular follow-ups were scheduled every three months to track his progress. After six months, Samir lost 6 kg, improved his physical stamina, and experienced fewer chest discomfort episodes. His cholesterol levels improved, and his blood pressure stabilized, leaving him feeling more energetic and motivated to maintain a healthier lifestyle.
