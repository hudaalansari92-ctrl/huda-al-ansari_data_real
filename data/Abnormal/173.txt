A 48-year-old male named Tom worked as an executive in a high-stress corporate environment located in an urban area known for poor air quality. He presented with complaints of anxiety, palpitations, and chest discomfort.
Tom described his chest discomfort as pressure that occurred mainly during stressful meetings or after long workdays. He also noted increased heart rate and difficulty sleeping due to stress.
While he did not have any diagnosed chronic diseases, he had elevated blood pressure readings during previous visits that he attributed to stress from his job.
His risk factors included high levels of occupational stress, long working hours that promoted a sedentary lifestyle, poor dietary habits (frequent takeout meals), exposure to outdoor pollutants from heavy traffic near his office, and a family history of heart disease (his father had CAD).
After evaluation revealed significant coronary artery disease with evidence of acute thrombosis risk due to vasoconstriction linked to pollution exposure, Tom was placed on medication for hypertension management. He was advised on stress management techniques and encouraged to make healthier lifestyle choices.
