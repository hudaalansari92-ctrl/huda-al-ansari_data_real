A 70-year-old male named George lived in an industrial area with high levels of air pollution due to nearby factories. He presented to the hospital with severe chest pain and shortness of breath.
George described his chest pain as a crushing sensation that radiated to his left arm. He also reported episodes of dizziness and fatigue, especially after walking short distances. He had experienced these symptoms intermittently for several months.
He had a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, both of which were poorly managed. He was also diagnosed with chronic bronchitis, which he attributed to years of living in a polluted environment.
His risk factors included long-term exposure to air pollutants such as particulate matter (PM2.5) and nitrogen dioxide (NO2), a sedentary lifestyle, and a family history of heart disease (his father had a heart attack at age 60). He also had elevated levels of inflammatory markers due to his chronic bronchitis.
Diagnostic tests revealed significant coronary artery disease with evidence of plaque rupture. George was treated with angioplasty and stenting. He was educated about the risks associated with living in polluted environments and encouraged to improve his lifestyle through diet and exercise.
