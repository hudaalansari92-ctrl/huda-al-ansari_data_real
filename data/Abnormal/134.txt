A 48-year-old male named Tom worked as an executive in a high-pressure environment. He sought medical attention for increased anxiety and occasional chest discomfort after particularly long work weeks.
Tom described the chest discomfort as tightness that would occur mainly after extremely busy days or weeks filled with back-to-back meetings. He also reported feelings of anxiety and irritability.
His risk factors included working more than 55 hours per week consistently, high job demands with low control over his schedule, poor dietary habits due to reliance on fast food during busy days, and a family history of heart disease (his uncle had a heart attack at age 60).
After evaluation showed signs consistent with early-stage CAD, Tom was advised on lifestyle modifications, including stress management techniques, dietary changes, and regular exercise. He was also encouraged to seek support for managing work-related stress effectively.
