A 55-year-old construction worker with a history of CAD experienced sudden chest pain and arm numbness while working at a site with significant dust and air pollution. Although his CAD had been controlled with medication, including statins and antiplatelet drugs, the physical demands of his job, combined with pollution exposure, seemed to exacerbate his condition. An angiogram showed a clot in one of his coronary arteries, which was promptly treated with thrombolytic therapy. His cardiologist explained that airborne pollutants may have triggered increased coagulation, leading to clot formation within his already vulnerable arteries. He was advised to consider job modifications, wear protective masks on-site, and maintain regular follow-ups to monitor his heart health.
