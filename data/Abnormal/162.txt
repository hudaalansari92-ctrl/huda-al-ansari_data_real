A 57-year-old man with a long-standing history of coronary artery disease (CAD) noticed a worsening of his angina symptoms after relocating to an area with high levels of particulate matter (PM2.5) due to nearby industrial activity. Despite adhering to his treatment regimen, which included statins and anti-anginal medications, he experienced more frequent chest discomfort and fatigue. His cardiologist suspected that chronic exposure to PM2.5 was inducing oxidative stress and systemic inflammation, leading to endothelial dysfunction that aggravated his CAD. He was advised to avoid outdoor activities during high pollution hours, use an air purifier at home, and continue with close cardiac monitoring to manage his CAD progression.
