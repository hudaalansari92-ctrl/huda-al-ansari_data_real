A 50-year-old female patient with a diagnosis of rheumatoid arthritis (RA) presented to the emergency department with severe chest pain and shortness of breath. Despite being relatively young and having no traditional cardiovascular risk factors such as obesity or diabetes, her medical history revealed a long-standing battle with RA, which was characterized by chronic inflammation and elevated inflammatory markers, including high levels of C-reactive protein (CRP). Over the years, she had experienced multiple flare-ups that required corticosteroid treatment, further exacerbating her cardiovascular risk. Upon evaluation, imaging studies showed significant coronary artery disease (CAD) with multiple lesions in her coronary arteries, indicating accelerated atherosclerosis. Additionally, the patient reported high levels of stress and anxiety related to her chronic illness, which contributed to her overall cardiovascular risk profile.
