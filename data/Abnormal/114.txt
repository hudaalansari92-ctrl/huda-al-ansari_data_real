A 59-year-old woman with a long-standing history of inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), specifically Crohn's disease, arrived at the clinic reporting frequent chest discomfort and fatigue. In addition to her Crohn's disease, which involved chronic inflammation, she had traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), including type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and obesity. Although she was on medications to manage her Crohn's and traditional risk factors, her symptoms prompted further investigation. An ECG and coronary angiography revealed significant CAD with a 70% blockage in one of her main coronary arteries, necessitating stent placement. Her cardiologist explained that the combination of traditional risk factors like diabetes, hypertension, and obesity, along with the chronic systemic inflammation from Crohn's disease, had likely accelerated the development of her CAD. She was advised to closely monitor both her Crohn's and cardiovascular health, adhere to a heart-healthy diet, and manage her diabetes and blood pressure to prevent further complications.
