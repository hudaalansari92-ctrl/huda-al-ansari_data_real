A 55-year-old woman with a history of coronary artery disease (CAD) and previous stent placement visited her cardiologist after experiencing recurring chest tightness and fatigue during her daily commute through a congested urban area. She had no new changes in her lifestyle, diet, or physical activity levels but reported that her symptoms flared up when exposed to heavy traffic. Further examination revealed no new blockages; however, her cardiologist suspected that acute vasoconstriction induced by air pollution was aggravating her existing CAD, causing periodic reductions in blood flow. She was advised to wear an N95 mask, limit outdoor exposure during high pollution times, and consider alternate commuting routes. Additional anti-anginal medication was prescribed to help manage her symptoms during unavoidable exposure to pollution.
