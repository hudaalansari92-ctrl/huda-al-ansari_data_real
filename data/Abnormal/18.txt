Tom, a 60-year-old farmer living in a rural area, spends most of his day working on his land, but his physical activity is limited to basic farm tasks. The community he lives in has few recreational spaces or fitness centers, making it difficult for him to engage in regular exercise. Additionally, the nearest grocery store is far from his home and offers mostly processed foods and fatty meats, with little access to fresh fruits or vegetables. Due to the convenience and cost, Tom's diet is high in red meat, fried foods, and sugary snacks. Over the years, this unhealthy diet and lack of consistent physical activity led to significant weight gain. He becomes obese and is eventually diagnosed with type 2 diabetes. Despite his diabetes diagnosis, Tom struggles to change his diet, and his condition remains poorly controlled. His high blood sugar levels and excess weight contribute to the buildup of plaque in his arteries, significantly increasing his risk for coronary artery disease (CAD). When he starts experiencing chest pain and fatigue, a doctor informs him that his long-term lifestyle habits have placed him at serious risk of a heart attack.
