A 67-year-old woman sought emergency care for prolonged chest pain. She had a history of hypertension, diabetes, and dyslipidemia and was a smoker.
In April 2009, she experienced chest pain diagnosed as a myocardial infarction.
Coronary angiography revealed 70% obstruction in the right coronary artery, anterior descending artery, and circumflex artery. A left ventriculogram indicated apical akinesia and an intracavitary thrombus.
The echocardiogram showed severe left ventricular dysfunction with a 28% ejection fraction.
After initial stabilization, she remained asymptomatic until October 2009, when she suffered a cerebrovascular accident leading to motor impairment. Clinical treatment was adjusted based on her evolving condition and coronary anatomy.
