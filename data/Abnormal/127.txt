A 72-year-old female presented with leg pain during exertion, which had progressively worsened over the past few months. She had been diagnosed with peripheral artery disease (PAD) two years prior but had not undergone any cardiac evaluations until now.
The patient experienced intermittent claudication in her calves when walking short distances. She also reported occasional chest discomfort that she attributed to anxiety rather than cardiac issues.
Her risk factors included a history of smoking (quit 10 years ago), hyperlipidemia, diabetes mellitus type 2, and a family history of cardiovascular diseases.
An angiogram revealed significant stenosis in both the right and left coronary arteries. The patient underwent lifestyle modifications, including supervised exercise therapy for her PAD and percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) for her CAD. She was educated on managing her cardiovascular risk factors effectively.
