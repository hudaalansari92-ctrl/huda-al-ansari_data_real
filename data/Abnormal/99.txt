A 45-year-old male patient with a history of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) presented with chest pain and was diagnosed with coronary artery disease (CAD) despite having no conventional risk factors such as hypertension, diabetes, or hyperlipidemia. His medical history included significant chronic inflammation due to SLE, which is known to elevate the risk of cardiovascular events. The patient was also experiencing symptoms of depression and anxiety, contributing to psychosocial stress, another nontraditional risk factor. He reported a sedentary lifestyle and poor dietary habits, which further exacerbated his condition. Upon evaluation, imaging studies revealed significant coronary artery plaque formation, leading to a diagnosis of CAD.
