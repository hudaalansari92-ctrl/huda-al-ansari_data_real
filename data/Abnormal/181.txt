Layla, a 65-year-old woman, was diagnosed with coronary artery disease with a minor heart attack due to the narrowing of the coronary arteries. Layla has a family history of heart disease, as her father died of a heart attack at the age of 70, and her mother suffers from diabetes. Although she is a non-smoker, she suffers from obesity (body mass index of 34) and a sedentary lifestyle, with a long history of high blood cholesterol levels (20 years). Chronic diseases included hypertension for 18 years and type 2 diabetes for 10 years. The symptoms began to appear as chest pain during simple activities and shortness of breath, and her condition worsened when she felt sharp chest pain that lasted more than 15 minutes, prompting her to go to the emergency room. The tests revealed myocardial ischemia and a minor heart attack, and the cardiac catheterization showed a 75% narrowing in the right coronary artery and a 50% narrowing in the left anterior descending coronary artery. She received emergency treatment that included clot-dissolving medications and a therapeutic catheterization with the placement of a stent in the right coronary artery. The treatment included improving her lifestyle by following a healthy diet, exercising regularly, and a plan for gradual weight loss. Medications were also prescribed to control cholesterol, blood pressure, and sugar levels, in addition to antiplatelet drugs. She enrolled in a cardiac rehabilitation program to enhance her health condition. After 6 months, she showed significant improvement; her cholesterol and blood pressure levels decreased, she lost 7 kilograms of weight, and she regained her ability to perform daily activities without pain.
