Ahmed, a 55-year-old man who works as a company manager, was diagnosed with coronary artery disease due to coronary artery stenosis. Ahmed has a family history of heart disease, as his father died of a heart attack at the age of 60, and his mother suffers from high blood pressure. On a personal level, Ahmed has been a smoker for 30 years, consuming a pack of cigarettes daily, is overweight (with a body mass index of 30), and does not exercise regularly. He was also diagnosed with high blood pressure 10 years ago and type 2 diabetes 5 years ago. Ahmed began to feel chest pain when exerting himself physically, such as climbing stairs or walking long distances, in addition to increased fatigue and shortness of breath. The tests, including an electrocardiogram, stress test, and cardiac catheterization, revealed a 70% narrowing in the left anterior descending coronary artery. Ahmed was treated through a comprehensive program that included lifestyle changes such as quitting smoking, following a healthy diet, and exercising regularly, in addition to medications like statins to lower cholesterol, beta-blockers and Angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors for blood pressure, and aspirin for stroke prevention, along with adjustments to diabetes medications. Ahmed also underwent the placement of a stent to improve blood flow. After 6 months, follow-up showed a significant improvement in his condition, with reduced cholesterol and blood pressure levels, an improved ability to perform daily activities without pain, and he quit smoking and lost some excess weight. Ahmed continued with medical follow-ups and a healthy lifestyle, which confirms the importance of early detection and following a healthy lifestyle in preventing complications of coronary artery disease.
