Linda, a 55-year-old woman, had been living with type 2 diabetes for more than 20 years. Her diabetes was poorly controlled, and she also had high blood pressure and high cholesterol. Despite her doctors' recommendations, Linda found it difficult to manage her diet and weight. She began experiencing episodes of chest discomfort and palpitations, especially after walking or exerting herself, but ignored the symptoms, thinking they were related to her diabetes. Over time, these symptoms worsened, and one evening, after climbing a flight of stairs, she experienced intense chest pain radiating to her back and shortness of breath. She was taken to the emergency department, where an ECG and blood tests confirmed she had suffered a heart attack. A coronary angiogram revealed multiple significant blockages in her coronary arteries, with a 90% blockage in the left circumflex artery. Due to her diabetes and the advanced nature of the blockages, Linda underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. Post-procedure, she was placed on a comprehensive medical regimen, including insulin to control her blood sugar, statins for her cholesterol, and blood pressure medication. Linda also began working with a dietitian to improve her diet and a physical therapist to incorporate regular exercise into her routine. The combination of medical therapy and lifestyle changes helped Linda reduce her risk of future heart problems.
