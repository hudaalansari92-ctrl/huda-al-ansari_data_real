Tom, a 58-year-old man with a 30-year history of smoking, had been diagnosed with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) five years ago. Over the past several months, he began experiencing chest pain and worsening shortness of breath, which initially seemed related to his COPD. However, the intensity of his chest pain raised concerns about his heart health. Blood tests showed elevated troponin levels, and his ECG revealed ST-segment depression, indicating ischemia. An echocardiogram showed reduced left ventricular function with an ejection fraction of 40%. A coronary angiogram later revealed multiple blockages, including a 90% blockage in the right coronary artery (RCA). Tom was diagnosed with coronary artery disease (CAD) alongside his COPD. He underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement, and his symptoms improved after the procedure. He was started on medications to manage both his heart disease and COPD, including statins, beta-blockers, and bronchodilators.
