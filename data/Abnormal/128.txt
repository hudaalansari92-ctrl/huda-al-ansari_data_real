A 60-year-old female with a history of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) presented for evaluation due to fatigue and chest discomfort that had become more pronounced over the past few months. She had been managing her condition with immunosuppressive therapy but expressed concerns about her cardiovascular health due to her chronic inflammatory disease.
The patient reported persistent fatigue, joint pain, and episodes of chest tightness that occurred during physical activity or stress. She also experienced palpitations occasionally.
Her risk factors included chronic inflammation due to SLE, prolonged corticosteroid use, high cholesterol levels, and a family history of heart disease.
A cardiac evaluation indicated significant coronary artery disease, including lesions in multiple vessels. The patient was started on aggressive medical management for her CAD, including antiplatelet therapy and statins. She continued her treatment for SLE while being monitored closely for cardiovascular events.
