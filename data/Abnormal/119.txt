A 72-year-old male with a long history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) presented to the emergency department with worsening shortness of breath, persistent cough, and chest discomfort that intensified during exertion. He had a significant smoking history, having smoked one pack a day for over 30 years before quitting five years ago.
His risk factors included a family history of heart disease and a sedentary lifestyle. He was also overweight, with a BMI of 29.
An ECG showed signs of ischemia, and a subsequent coronary angiogram revealed significant stenosis in the right coronary artery.
The patient underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) to alleviate the blockage. He was started on bronchodilators for his COPD and was advised on lifestyle changes to improve both his respiratory and cardiovascular health.
