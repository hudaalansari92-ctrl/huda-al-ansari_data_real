A 50-year-old female named Sarah worked as a nurse in a hospital located in an urban area known for high levels of air pollution. She came in complaining of chest discomfort and fatigue after long shifts.
Sarah described her chest discomfort as pressure that occurred mainly during stressful shifts or after long hours at work. She also reported increased shortness of breath when walking quickly or climbing stairs.
While she did not have any diagnosed chronic diseases, she had elevated blood pressure readings during previous visits that she attributed to stress from her job.
Her risk factors included working irregular hours (shift work), high levels of occupational stress, poor dietary habits due to long shifts, and exposure to outdoor pollutants from heavy traffic near the hospital.
After evaluation revealed significant coronary artery disease, Sarah was started on medications for her cholesterol and blood pressure management. She was also referred for stress management programs and encouraged to exercise regularly.
