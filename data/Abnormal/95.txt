A 58-year-old man, who had lived and worked for over three decades in an industrial area with high air pollution levels, was admitted to the hospital after experiencing persistent chest pain and shortness of breath. Despite having no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), he was a non-smoker, physically active, and maintained a healthy weight; he had been exposed for years to fine particulate matter (PM2.5) and other pollutants from nearby factories and heavy traffic. Diagnostic tests, including coronary angiography, revealed multiple significant blockages in his coronary arteries, requiring immediate stent placement. His cardiologist explained that his long-term exposure to air pollution likely contributed to inflammation in his blood vessels, accelerating the atherosclerotic process despite the absence of other risk factors. Following the procedure, he was advised on lifestyle modifications, including minimizing his exposure to polluted areas and using protective measures when possible to help manage his heart health going forward.
