A 52-year-old man with a long history of psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis presented to the emergency department with chest pain and shortness of breath. Despite having no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), he was non-diabetic, had normal cholesterol levels, and did not smoke, and he had been dealing with chronic inflammation due to his autoimmune condition for over 15 years. His family history was also negative for cardiovascular disease. Upon evaluation, an electrocardiogram (ECG) and subsequent coronary angiography revealed significant coronary artery disease, with multiple blockages requiring stent placement. His cardiologist attributed the premature CAD to chronic systemic inflammation from his psoriatic disease, which likely accelerated atherosclerosis in the absence of typical risk factors. Post-procedure, he was prescribed medications to manage both his heart condition and inflammation and advised on the importance of regular follow-up to monitor both his cardiovascular and inflammatory health.
