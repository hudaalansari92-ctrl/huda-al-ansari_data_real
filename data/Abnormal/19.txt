Mary, a 50-year-old woman living in a rural town, works two low-paying jobs to support her family. Despite her hard work, her income barely covers basic living expenses, leaving little room for regular healthcare visits or healthier food options. The cost of fresh fruits, vegetables, and lean meats is higher in her area, so she relies on cheaper, processed foods high in salt and fat to feed her family. Over the years, Mary started experiencing symptoms of high blood pressure and high cholesterol, such as frequent headaches and occasional dizziness, but she ignored them because she could not afford regular doctor visits or medications. As her conditions go untreated, the damage to her arteries worsens. One day, after finishing a long shift at work, Mary feels severe chest pain and collapses. She is rushed to the nearest hospital, where doctors confirm she has suffered a heart attack. Her high cholesterol and hypertension, both of which had been unmanaged for years, were major contributors to the event. If Mary had access to affordable healthcare and healthier food, her conditions might have been detected and treated earlier, potentially preventing the heart attack.
