Martha, a 62-year-old caregiver, spent her days providing full-time care for her elderly mother, which was both physically and emotionally demanding. Over the past year, she has experienced increasing episodes of chest pain and fatigue, especially after lifting her mother or performing household tasks. Martha lived in a small apartment and found little time for self-care due to her caregiving responsibilities, often eating irregularly and relying on processed convenience foods. Her symptoms included a tight, pressure-like sensation in her chest that radiated to her neck and shoulders. Despite her pain, she continued to dismiss it as stress. After a particularly intense episode of chest pain while carrying groceries, she sought medical attention. Given her chronic stress and poor diet, her doctor suspected coronary artery disease (CAD).
Further evaluation revealed multiple blockages in her coronary arteries. Martha underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement, which alleviated her symptoms. Post-procedure, she was encouraged to find support for her caregiving duties, adopt a healthier diet, and incorporate light exercise into her routine to improve her heart health.
