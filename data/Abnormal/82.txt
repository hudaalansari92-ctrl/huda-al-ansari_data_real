Linda, a 60-year-old woman, had been managing high cholesterol for over 20 years with inconsistent medication use and minimal lifestyle changes. Over the past several months, she began experiencing chest tightness, especially during activities like walking uphill or carrying groceries. The pain was usually accompanied by shortness of breath and lightheadedness. Linda initially dismissed the symptoms as fatigue but grew concerned when the episodes became more frequent and occurred even at rest. Given her long-standing history of high cholesterol, her doctor suspected coronary artery disease (CAD). Further evaluation confirmed that Linda had significant narrowing of her coronary arteries due to plaque buildup. She underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement to improve blood flow. Following the procedure, Linda's symptoms improved, and she was placed on a strict regimen of statins, aspirin, and lifestyle changes, including a low-cholesterol diet and regular physical activity.
