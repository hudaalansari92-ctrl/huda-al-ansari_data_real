A 72-year-old male named Tom lived in an area with high levels of air pollution due to nearby factories. He presented with new-onset chest pain and increasing shortness of breath during physical activity.
Tom described his chest pain as tightness that worsened during physical exertion or when the air quality was particularly poor. He also experienced fatigue after minimal activity.
Tom had a history of asthma, which he attributed to his living environment. He also had well-controlled hypertension but was concerned about his heart health due to recent symptoms.
Risk Factors: His risk factors included long-term exposure to air pollution from industrial emissions, being overweight (BMI of 28), and having a family history of heart disease (his father had CAD).
After diagnostic testing confirmed significant coronary artery disease, Tom was placed on appropriate medications to manage his condition. He was advised on the importance of reducing pollution exposure and encouraged to adopt healthier lifestyle choices.
