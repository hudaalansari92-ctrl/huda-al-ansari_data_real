A 60-year-old female named Karen with rheumatoid arthritis presented for follow-up regarding her condition. She expressed concerns about her cardiovascular health due to her chronic inflammatory disease. Karen reported increased fatigue and joint pain but denied any chest pain. However, she mentioned experiencing occasional palpitations during flare-ups of her arthritis.
Her risk factors included chronic inflammation from rheumatoid arthritis, prolonged use of corticosteroids for treatment, obesity (BMI of 29), and a family history of heart disease (her brother had CAD).
After comprehensive evaluations indicated early signs of coronary artery disease, Karen was started on aggressive management for both her rheumatoid arthritis and cardiovascular health. She was educated about the importance of controlling inflammation and maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
