A 58-year-old female named Linda presented for follow-up regarding her rheumatoid arthritis. She lived near a highway with heavy traffic and expressed concerns about her cardiovascular health due to her chronic inflammatory condition.
Linda reported increased fatigue, joint pain, and episodes of chest tightness during flare-ups of her arthritis. She also experienced palpitations occasionally but no significant chest pain at rest.
Chronic Diseases: Her chronic inflammatory condition (rheumatoid arthritis) puts her at higher risk for cardiovascular issues due to systemic inflammation. She had been taking corticosteroids, which can contribute to cardiovascular risk.
Living near a busy highway exposed her to elevated levels of air pollution, particularly from vehicle emissions. Additionally, she had a family history of heart disease; her mother had suffered from CAD.
After comprehensive evaluations indicated early signs of coronary artery disease, Linda was started on aggressive management for both her rheumatoid arthritis and cardiovascular health. She was educated about the importance of managing inflammation and reducing exposure to environmental pollutants.
