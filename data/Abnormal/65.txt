A 55-year-old female presented for a routine check-up after being diagnosed with hyperlipidemia. Her lipid profile showed elevated LDL cholesterol levels, and she had a family history of heart disease. She reported experiencing episodes of chest pain during exertion. An ECG showed signs of ischemia, prompting further investigation through coronary angiography, which revealed significant blockages in multiple coronary arteries. The patient was started on statin therapy to manage her cholesterol levels and underwent coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG) to restore blood flow. With lifestyle modifications and medication adherence, she was able to manage her CAD effectively.
