A 70-year-old female presented with intermittent claudication, experiencing leg pain during walking that improved with rest. She had been diagnosed with peripheral artery disease (PAD) two years prior but had not undergone any cardiac evaluations until now.
Her risk factors included a history of smoking (quit 10 years ago), hyperlipidemia, and a sedentary lifestyle. She also reported a family history of cardiovascular diseases.
An ankle-brachial index test confirmed PAD. An ECG showed signs consistent with ischemia, leading to coronary angiography that revealed significant stenosis in both the right and left coronary arteries.
The patient underwent lifestyle modifications, including a supervised exercise program for her PAD and PCI for her CAD. She was educated on managing her cardiovascular risk factors effectively.
