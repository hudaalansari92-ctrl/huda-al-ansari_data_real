A 55-year-old female named Maria had rheumatoid arthritis and lived in an area with poor air quality due to nearby highways. She presented with worsening joint pain and new-onset chest discomfort.
Maria reported tightness in her chest that occurred during physical activity or when the air quality was particularly poor. During flare-ups, she also experienced increased fatigue and difficulty breathing.
Her chronic inflammatory condition made her more susceptible to cardiovascular issues due to systemic inflammation. She was on corticosteroids for her rheumatologic arthritis.
Living near heavy traffic exposed her to elevated levels of particulate matter (PM2.5) and other pollutants. Additionally, she had a family history of heart disease; her mother had suffered from CAD.
After comprehensive evaluations indicated significant coronary artery disease with evidence of thrombosis formation, Maria was started on aggressive management for both her rheumatoid arthritis and cardiovascular health. She was placed on anticoagulants alongside antiplatelet therapy and educated about managing inflammation while minimizing exposure to environmental pollutants.
