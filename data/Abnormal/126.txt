A 65-year-old male diagnosed with obstructive sleep apnea presented to the cardiology clinic after experiencing recurrent episodes of chest pain and fatigue. He had been non-compliant with his CPAP therapy due to discomfort.
The patient reported excessive daytime sleepiness, loud snoring, and morning headaches. He also experienced chest pain that occurred at rest and during physical activity.
His risk factors included obesity (BMI of 32), a family history of heart disease, hypertension, which was poorly controlled, and a sedentary lifestyle.
A cardiac evaluation revealed significant coronary artery disease, including lesions in multiple vessels. The patient was started on medications for hypertension and underwent PCI. He was also referred back to sleep medicine for better management of his sleep apnea, emphasizing the importance of compliance with CPAP therapy.
