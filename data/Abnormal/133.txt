A 55-year-old female named Karen had a history of lupus erythematosus. She visited her physician for routine follow-up but expressed concerns about her heart health due to her chronic condition.
Karen reported fatigue, joint pain, and occasional episodes of chest tightness during flare-ups but denied any significant chest pain or discomfort at rest.
Her risk factors included chronic inflammation from lupus, prolonged use of corticosteroids, obesity (BMI of 29), and a family history of heart disease (her mother had CAD). She also experienced high levels of stress related to managing her illness.
After comprehensive evaluations indicated early signs of CAD, Karen was started on aggressive management for both her lupus and cardiovascular health. She was educated about the importance of controlling inflammation and maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
