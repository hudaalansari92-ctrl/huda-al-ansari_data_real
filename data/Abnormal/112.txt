A 55-year-old woman with a 20-year history of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), an autoimmune disease marked by chronic inflammation, presented with chest pain and fatigue during daily activities. In addition to her lupus, she had traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), including high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, and a family history of heart disease. Despite her efforts to manage these conditions, her SLE added a nontraditional risk factor due to the persistent inflammation affecting her blood vessels. Coronary angiography confirmed advanced CAD with severe blockages in multiple arteries, necessitating stent placement. Her cardiologist explained that the combination of traditional risk factors like hypertension, diabetes, and family history, alongside chronic inflammation from lupus, had likely accelerated the development of her CAD. Post-procedure, she was advised to continue managing her lupus carefully, monitor her diabetes and blood pressure closely, and maintain a heart-healthy lifestyle to reduce further complications.
