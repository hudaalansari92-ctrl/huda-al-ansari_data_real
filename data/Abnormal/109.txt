A 48-year-old man with a 12-year history of vasculitis, an autoimmune condition causing inflammation of the blood vessels, visited his doctor due to persistent chest pain and shortness of breath, especially during light exertion. Although he had no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), he was a non-smoker, maintained a healthy weight, and had normal blood pressure and cholesterol levels, and his vasculitis had led to chronic vascular inflammation, requiring ongoing treatment to manage flare-ups. Concerned by his symptoms, his physician ordered coronary angiography, which revealed significant CAD with a 65% blockage in a major coronary artery, necessitating stent placement. The cardiologist explained that chronic inflammation from vasculitis likely accelerated atherosclerosis, increasing his risk of CAD despite the absence of typical risk factors. Post-procedure, he was advised to maintain strict control of his vasculitis, adopt a heart-healthy lifestyle, and schedule regular cardiovascular follow-ups to monitor his heart health and reduce future complications.
