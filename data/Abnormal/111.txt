A 60-year-old man with a long-standing history of rheumatoid arthritis (RA), an autoimmune disease causing chronic joint inflammation, presented to his cardiologist with worsening chest pain and shortness of breath. In addition to his RA, he had traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD): he was a former smoker, had high blood pressure, and elevated cholesterol levels, all of which were managed with medication. However, the persistent systemic inflammation from his RA added a nontraditional risk factor, further elevating his risk for heart disease. An ECG and coronary angiography revealed significant CAD with multiple blockages, requiring stent placement. His cardiologist explained that the combination of traditional factors like smoking, hypertension, and high cholesterol, along with chronic inflammation from RA, likely accelerated the progression of his CAD. After the procedure, he was advised to continue managing his RA aggressively, adhere to a heart-healthy diet, and prioritize regular follow-ups to monitor both his autoimmune and cardiovascular health.
