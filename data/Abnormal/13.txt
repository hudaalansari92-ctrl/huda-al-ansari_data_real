A 52-year-old male executive at a multinational corporation presented with chest pain and shortness of breath. He had a history of hypertension and was a former smoker.
Occupational Stress: The patient reported high job demands, long work hours, and limited control over his work. He frequently worked late nights and weekends to meet deadlines.
Clinical Findings: Diagnostic tests revealed significant blockages in the left anterior descending and right coronary arteries. An echocardiogram showed a reduced ejection fraction.
Management: The patient underwent coronary artery bypass grafting. He was counseled on lifestyle modifications, including stress management techniques, regular exercise, and a heart-healthy diet. Medications were prescribed to control blood pressure and cholesterol.
Outcome: After making significant changes to his work schedule and incorporating stress reduction strategies, the patient's symptoms improved. However, he remained at increased risk for future cardiac events due to the extent of his coronary artery disease.
