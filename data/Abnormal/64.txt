A 67-year-old male with a long-standing history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) presented to the emergency department with worsening dyspnea and chest discomfort. He had a significant smoking history and was on bronchodilator therapy. Given his respiratory symptoms and risk factors, he underwent a comprehensive cardiac evaluation, including an electrocardiogram (ECG) and coronary angiography. The angiogram revealed significant stenosis in the left anterior descending artery, confirming the presence of coronary artery disease. The patient was treated with a combination of bronchodilators and underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) to address the CAD. His management plan included both respiratory therapy and cardiac rehabilitation to improve his overall health.
