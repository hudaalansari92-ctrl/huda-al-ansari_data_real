Emma, a 38-year-old mother of two, lives in a major city and works full-time while juggling her family responsibilities. With a demanding work schedule and limited time to prepare home-cooked meals, she often relies on fast food and processed meals to feed herself and her children. These meals are convenient but typically high in saturated fats, salt, and sugar. Over the years, Emma's diet has become heavily based on burgers, fries, and sugary beverages, leading to significant weight gain. Despite noticing the gradual increase in her weight and feeling more fatigued, she dismisses her symptoms, attributing them to her busy lifestyle. After a few years, Emma is diagnosed with prediabetes, a warning sign that her body is struggling to manage blood sugar levels. However, she continues her unhealthy eating habits due to the convenience and affordability of fast food in the city. Eventually, Emma began experiencing frequent chest discomfort and shortness of breath. After visiting her doctor, she undergoes tests and is diagnosed with coronary artery disease (CAD). Her years of consuming high-fat, high-sugar foods, combined with weight gain and prediabetes, have significantly increased her risk of heart disease. Emma's fast-paced urban lifestyle, reliance on unhealthy food, and lack of time for exercise all contributed to the development of CAD.
