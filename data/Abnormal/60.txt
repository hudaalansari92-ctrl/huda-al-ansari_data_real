Maria, a 55-year-old woman with poorly controlled type 2 diabetes, presented with progressive chest pain and fatigue that she initially attributed to stress from her job as a schoolteacher. Her diabetes had been unmanaged for years, with her HbA1c regularly above 9%, and she also struggled with obesity. Over time, the elevated blood sugar levels damaged her blood vessels, significantly increasing her risk of developing coronary artery disease. After experiencing severe chest pain while walking upstairs, Maria was rushed to the emergency room. Blood tests showed markedly elevated troponin levels (TnI: 2.2 ng/mL), and her ECG revealed ST-segment elevation, consistent with a myocardial infarction (heart attack). An echocardiogram revealed a reduced ejection fraction of 40%, and coronary angiography confirmed an 85% blockage in the right coronary artery. Maria underwent urgent PCI and was placed on insulin therapy, statins, and antihypertensive medications. She was also referred to a diabetes management program to better control her blood sugar, which significantly reduced her risk of future cardiac events.
