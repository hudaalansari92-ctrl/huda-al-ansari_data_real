A 52-year-old female named Linda visited her primary care physician for routine blood work. She was concerned about her weight and overall health, as she had been feeling increasingly fatigued.
Linda reported occasional chest discomfort during physical activity, which she dismissed as being out of shape. She also experienced shortness of breath when climbing stairs and noted persistent fatigue throughout the day.
Her risk factors included obesity (BMI of 32), a sedentary lifestyle due to her desk job as an accountant, and a family history of cardiovascular disease (her mother had heart disease). She also had elevated cholesterol levels but was not on any medication.
After further evaluation revealed significant blockages in her coronary arteries, Linda was diagnosed with CAD. She was started on statin therapy and referred to a nutritionist for dietary counseling. She was encouraged to engage in regular physical activity to help manage her weight and reduce her risk.
