A 63-year-old man with a sedentary lifestyle presented to his doctor with complaints of chest discomfort and shortness of breath after minimal exertion, such as climbing stairs. Although he had no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), he was a non-smoker, maintained a normal weight, and had normal blood pressure and cholesterol levels; he had led an extremely inactive lifestyle for many years, spending most of his days sitting at a desk and avoiding physical activity. Concerned about his symptoms, his doctor conducted an ECG and coronary angiography, revealing significant CAD with a 65% blockage in one of his main coronary arteries, necessitating a stent. His cardiologist explained that his low physical activity levels likely contributed to reduced cardiovascular fitness and increased vascular stiffness, which can accelerate CAD development even without other risk factors. Post-procedure, he was advised to gradually increase his physical activity with daily walks and light exercise, follow a heart-healthy diet, and attend regular cardiovascular check-ups to prevent further complications.
