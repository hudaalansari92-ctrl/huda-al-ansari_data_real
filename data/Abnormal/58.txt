Steve, a 60-year-old man with a long-standing history of obesity, had been diagnosed with metabolic syndrome, which included central obesity, hypertension, high cholesterol, and insulin resistance. He worked a sedentary job and had struggled with weight management for most of his adult life. Obesity and metabolic syndrome are major risk factors for coronary artery disease. Metabolic syndrome involves a cluster of conditions that increase the risk of atherosclerosis and insulin resistance, contributing to the development of coronary heart disease. Steve experienced increasing episodes of chest pain, particularly after meals or walking short distances. Blood tests revealed elevated troponin levels (1.0 ng/mL), suggesting heart damage, and his lipid panel showed elevated LDL cholesterol (190 mg/dL) and triglycerides (250 mg/dL). His fasting blood glucose was 130 mg/dL, indicating prediabetes. An ECG revealed ST-segment depression, and an echocardiogram showed an ejection fraction of 45%. A coronary angiogram revealed an 80% blockage in his right coronary artery. Steve underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. He was started on a strict medication regimen, including statins, metformin for his insulin resistance, and antihypertensives to control his blood pressure. He also joined a weight management program and worked with a nutritionist to adopt a heart-healthy diet, significantly reducing his risk of future coronary events.
