A 50-year-old male named Mark, a corporate lawyer, presented to the clinic with complaints of chest tightness and frequent headaches. He worked long hours, often exceeding 60 hours a week, and reported that his job involved high levels of stress due to tight deadlines and demanding clients.
Mark described the chest tightness as a squeezing sensation that often occurred during stressful meetings. He also experienced fatigue and difficulty sleeping, which he attributed to work-related stress.
His risk factors included a family history of heart disease (his father had a heart attack at age 55), a high-stress work environment, and a sedentary lifestyle. He had poor dietary habits, often relying on takeout food during late nights at the office.
After undergoing diagnostic testing, Mark was diagnosed with CAD. He was started on medications to manage his cholesterol and blood pressure and was referred for cardiac rehabilitation. He was advised to adopt stress management techniques and improve his diet.
