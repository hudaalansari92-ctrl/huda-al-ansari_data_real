John, a 65-year-old man with a 20-year history of uncontrolled hypertension, lived a sedentary lifestyle and rarely sought medical care. Despite frequent warnings from his physician, John struggled to adhere to his medication regimen due to work stress and a poor diet high in salt and processed foods. Over time, the chronic high blood pressure led to thickening of his arterial walls, increasing his risk of atherosclerosis. John began experiencing chest pain and shortness of breath while gardening. Blood tests showed elevated troponin levels (TnI: 1.4 ng/mL), indicating heart muscle damage, and his ECG revealed ST-segment depression. An echocardiogram showed a reduced ejection fraction of 45%, and a coronary angiogram revealed a 75% blockage in the left anterior descending artery. John underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. He was started on a combination of blood pressure medications, statins, and aspirin, and advised to adopt a low-sodium diet and engage in regular exercise to prevent further cardiovascular complications.
