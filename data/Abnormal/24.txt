Carlos, a 50-year-old immigrant construction worker living in a large city, works long hours to provide for his family. Despite the availability of healthcare facilities in the city, Carlos avoids seeking medical care due to his lack of health insurance and concerns about the cost of treatment. He has experienced occasional chest pain and shortness of breath for several months, especially after physically demanding days on the construction site. However, worried about missing work and the financial burden of medical bills, he convinces himself the symptoms are temporary and manageable.
As his symptoms worsen, Carlos continues to delay medical attention, hoping the discomfort will pass. His physical labor adds strain to his already stressed cardiovascular system, but his fear of the cost and distrust of the healthcare system prevent him from seeking help. One afternoon, while working, he experiences intense chest pain and collapses. He is rushed to the emergency room, where doctors perform tests and discover that Carlos has advanced coronary artery disease (CAD), with significant blockages in his arteries. The doctors informed him that had he sought medical attention earlier, his condition could have been managed with medication and lifestyle changes. Now, however, he requires immediate interventions like angioplasty or even bypass surgery to restore blood flow to his heart. His delay in seeking care due to his lack of insurance and financial insecurity has allowed the disease to progress to a critical stage.
