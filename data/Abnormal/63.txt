Tom, a 57-year-old man with a long-standing history of obesity and metabolic syndrome, worked a desk job with little physical activity. He was diagnosed with metabolic syndrome, characterized by a combination of obesity, high blood pressure, elevated cholesterol, and insulin resistance. Over time, these conditions significantly increased his risk for coronary artery disease. Tom began experiencing intermittent chest pain and fatigue during mild exertion, such as walking short distances or climbing stairs. Blood tests revealed elevated troponin levels (TnI: 1.0 ng/mL), and his lipid profile showed elevated LDL cholesterol (190 mg/dL) and triglycerides (250 mg/dL). His ECG showed ST-segment depression, and an echocardiogram revealed an ejection fraction of 45%. A coronary angiogram identified an 80% blockage in the left anterior descending artery. Tom underwent PCI with stent placement and post-procedure, he was placed on statins, antihypertensive medications, and metformin to manage his insulin resistance. He also joined a weight-loss program and worked with a nutritionist to adopt a healthier lifestyle, significantly reducing his risk of future coronary events.
