A 54-year-old man with a family history of coronary artery disease (CAD) and a high polygenic risk score for CAD reported worsening chest pain and shortness of breath after moving to a highly polluted metropolitan area for work. His cardiovascular history included mild CAD, diagnosed five years earlier, which had been relatively stable. However, since relocating, he has experienced more frequent angina symptoms, particularly on high-pollution days. His cardiologist explained that his genetic susceptibility, combined with consistent air pollution exposure, likely intensified his CAD risk, accelerating his symptoms and progression of the disease. He was advised to take preventive measures, such as limiting outdoor exposure during peak pollution hours, using air purifiers at home, and attending regular follow-ups to monitor his heart health closely.
