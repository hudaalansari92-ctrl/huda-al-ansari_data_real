Khaled, a 52-year-old truck driver, was diagnosed with coronary artery disease due to the narrowing of the coronary arteries and multiple risk factors, such as chronic smoking, high blood pressure, and elevated cholesterol levels. Khaled works in an environment that imposes long hours of sitting and inactivity, heavily relying on fast food available at gas stations, leading to weight gain (BMI of 29) and lack of physical activity. Additionally, he lives in an area lacking green spaces, making it more difficult to exercise. He started experiencing chest pain while driving, especially in traffic or going uphill, and the symptoms worsened until he had to stop driving and call for an ambulance. The tests showed myocardial ischemia and elevated bad cholesterol, while the coronary angiography revealed a 60% stenosis in the left anterior descending coronary artery. He was treated through a comprehensive program that included quitting smoking, following a healthy diet, and performing simple exercises during breaks. He was also prescribed medications such as statins to lower cholesterol, beta-blockers for blood pressure, and aspirin for stroke prevention. The work environment was adjusted by reducing continuous driving hours and providing him with guidelines on choosing healthy meals while traveling. After 6 months of treatment, Khaled showed significant improvement with reduced cholesterol and blood pressure levels, stopped smoking, and lost some weight.
