Yassin, a 58-year-old retired engineer, has been living with chronic kidney disease (CKD) stage 3 for eight years due to long-standing hypertension and poorly controlled type 2 diabetes. His kidney function has progressively declined, with eGFR at 45 mL/min/1.73 m², creatinine at 2.1 mg/dL, and BUN at 32 mg/dL, indicating moderate kidney dysfunction. He also has dyslipidemia, with LDL cholesterol at 160 mg/dL and HDL at 35 mg/dL, and his blood pressure remains high at 150/95 mmHg despite medication. Over the past six months, he experienced frequent chest pain, shortness of breath, persistent fatigue, and leg swelling, especially after mild exertion. Concerned about these symptoms, he sought medical evaluation. Diagnostic tests revealed mild ST-segment depressions on ECG, left ventricular hypertrophy on echocardiogram, reduced exercise tolerance on a stress test, and significant arterial blockages, 75% in the LAD artery and 60% in the RCA on coronary angiography, leading to a diagnosis of coronary artery disease (CAD) exacerbated by CKD, hypertension, and dyslipidemia. His treatment plan included statins, beta-blockers, aspirin, clopidogrel, diuretics, and ACE inhibitors with close kidney function monitoring. He also underwent coronary angioplasty with stent placement to restore blood flow and was advised to follow a low-sodium, kidney-friendly diet and engage in light physical activity. After six months, his chest pain episodes decreased, blood pressure stabilized at 130/85 mmHg, LDL cholesterol dropped to 110 mg/dL, and kidney function remained stable.
