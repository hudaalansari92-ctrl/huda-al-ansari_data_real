Fatima, a 62-year-old woman and retired teacher, was diagnosed with coronary artery disease with a severe heart attack due to a blockage in the right coronary artery. Fatima has a family history of heart disease, as her mother suffers from diabetes and her father died of a stroke at the age of 65. Although she is a non-smoker, she suffers from obesity (body mass index of 32) and does not exercise regularly, and she has been diagnosed with hypertension and metabolic syndrome. The symptoms began with chest pain during simple daily activities, and the pain intensified sharply and lasted for more than 20 minutes, prompting her to go to the emergency room. The tests, including an electrocardiogram and elevated cardiac enzymes, revealed a heart attack. The cardiac catheterization revealed a complete blockage in the right coronary artery and a 50% narrowing in the left anterior descending coronary artery. She was treated with thrombolysis and an emergency catheterization with stent placement. The treatment included changing her lifestyle through a healthy diet, exercise, and a weight loss plan, in addition to medications such as statins, beta-blockers, and antiplatelets. She enrolled in a cardiac rehabilitation program to improve her condition. After 6 months, Fatima's condition improved significantly; her cholesterol and blood pressure levels decreased, she lost 8 kilograms of excess weight, and her ability to perform daily activities without pain improved. Her condition underscores the importance of prompt intervention in cases of heart attacks and the necessity of adhering to a healthy lifestyle to prevent the worsening of coronary artery disease.
