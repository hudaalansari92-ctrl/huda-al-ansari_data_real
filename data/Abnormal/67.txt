A 72-year-old female presented with leg pain during exertion, which had progressively worsened over the past few months. She had a history of peripheral artery disease (PAD) but denied any chest pain or other cardiac symptoms. Due to her PAD diagnosis and risk factors, she underwent a cardiac evaluation that included an ECG and coronary angiography. The angiogram revealed significant stenosis in the right coronary artery. The patient was treated for both her PAD and CAD through lifestyle modifications, medication management, and a recommendation for supervised exercise therapy. She successfully improved her symptoms and quality of life.
