an 88-Year-Old Female with Chronic CAD
An 88-year-old female presented with complaints of angina on moderate exertion. Her medical history included chronic coronary artery disease, atrial fibrillation, and previous cardiac interventions.
On August 22, 2011, coronary angiography revealed:
90% in-stent restenosis in the right coronary artery.
80% obstruction in the mid-anterior interventricular artery.
90% distal obstruction of the circumflex artery.
On September 15, 2011, laboratory results showed:
Hemoglobin: 12.1 g/dL
Creatinine: 1.11 mg/dL
Triglycerides: 223 mg/dL
Total cholesterol: 175 mg/dL
LDL-cholesterol: 83 mg/dL
Troponin: 0.064 ng/mL (indicating no significant myocardial necrosis)
The ECG revealed sinus rhythm with diffuse ventricular repolarization changes but no significant ST-segment elevation or depression.
The echocardiogram was performed to assess left ventricular function and wall motion abnormalities, confirming preserved left ventricular motility despite significant coronary artery obstructions.
The patient was placed on a regimen of antiplatelet therapy and statins, and lifestyle modifications were recommended to manage her CAD.
