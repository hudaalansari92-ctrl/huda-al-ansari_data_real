John, a 55-year-old office worker with a sedentary lifestyle, had been experiencing chest pain and tightness for several months, particularly when walking short distances or climbing stairs. The chest pain was often accompanied by shortness of breath and mild nausea, which left him feeling fatigued throughout the day. Despite these warning signs, John ignored the symptoms, thinking they were related to stress or lack of exercise. His physician, however, suspected coronary artery disease (CAD) due to John's sedentary lifestyle, poor dietary habits, and lack of physical activity. Upon further investigation, it was found that John had severe blockages in his coronary arteries. He underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI), during which multiple stents were placed to open the blocked arteries. Post-procedure, John's symptoms resolved, and he was advised to adopt a more active lifestyle, incorporating regular exercise and a healthier diet to prevent future cardiovascular events.
