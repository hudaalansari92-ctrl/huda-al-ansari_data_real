A 48-year-old male named David worked as an IT consultant in a high-stress urban environment. He presented with complaints of intermittent chest discomfort and increased fatigue over the past few months.
David described his chest discomfort as a dull ache that occurred occasionally during stressful work periods. He also reported feeling unusually tired after sitting at his desk for long hours without breaks.
His risk factors included a sedentary lifestyle due to prolonged hours in front of the computer, obesity (BMI of 31), high-stress levels related to project deadlines, and poor dietary choices (high in processed foods). He had elevated cholesterol levels but was not on any medication.
Diagnostic tests revealed significant coronary artery disease. David was started on statin therapy and advised to incorporate regular physical activity into his routine along with dietary changes to improve his overall cardiovascular health.
