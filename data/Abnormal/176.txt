John is a 55-year-old construction manager living near a major highway and several manufacturing plants, contributing to his exposure to air pollution. A significant family history of coronary artery disease, with multiple relatives experiencing heart attacks, has raised his concerns. John, a former smoker with type 2 diabetes, sought genetic testing after experiencing fatigue and palpitations, which revealed a high polygenic risk score for CAD. Comprehensive evaluations indicated moderate ischemia, and coronary angiography showed blockages in three major coronary arteries. His treatment plan focuses on lifestyle interventions, including dietary changes and exercise, alongside medications for diabetes and cholesterol management. John was advised to limit outdoor activities during high-pollution days and engage in indoor exercises instead.
