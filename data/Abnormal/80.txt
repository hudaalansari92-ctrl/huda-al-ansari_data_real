Susan, a 62-year-old woman with a strong family history of heart disease, experienced recurrent episodes of chest discomfort over the past year. The discomfort, which she described as a squeezing sensation in her chest, would occur during activities like gardening or walking, and would typically subside with rest. She also noted that she often felt fatigued and sometimes experienced dizziness. Despite her symptoms, Susan delayed seeking medical attention, attributing the chest pain to ageing and stress. However, after a particularly severe episode of chest pain that left her breathless, she went to see her doctor. Given her family history, age, and symptoms, Susan's doctor suspected coronary artery disease (CAD). After further evaluation, significant blockages in her coronary arteries were found. She underwent coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG) surgery to bypass the blocked arteries, and her recovery was successful. Post-surgery, Susan began a cardiac rehabilitation program and was advised to maintain a heart-healthy diet and exercise routine.
