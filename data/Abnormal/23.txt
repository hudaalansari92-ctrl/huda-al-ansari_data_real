Michael, a 47-year-old IT professional, works in a large city where he spends most of his day sitting at his desk, often for 8-10 hours straight. His job is demanding, leaving little time for physical activity, and his sedentary lifestyle becomes the norm. With the convenience of fast food restaurants near his office, Michael frequently opts for high-calorie, low-nutrient meals like burgers, pizza, and sugary snacks. Over the years, his weight steadily increases, and he becomes obese. Despite occasional thoughts of changing his habits, he struggles to find the time or motivation to exercise after long workdays.
As Michael's weight continues to rise, he begins experiencing symptoms such as frequent fatigue, excessive thirst, and blurred vision. A visit to his doctor confirms that he has developed type 2 diabetes, a condition closely linked to his obesity and sedentary lifestyle. Over time, the combination of diabetes, obesity, and poor dietary choices takes a toll on his cardiovascular health. He begins to experience chest discomfort and shortness of breath, and further tests reveal that Michael has coronary artery disease (CAD). His obesity, compounded by a lack of exercise and unhealthy eating habits, significantly increased his risk for heart disease, leading to the narrowing of his arteries and raising the potential for a heart attack.
