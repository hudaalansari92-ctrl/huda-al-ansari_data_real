A 45-year-old male presented to the emergency department with acute chest pain and diaphoresis. His risk factors included a family history of heart disease, smoking, and a sedentary lifestyle.
He reported severe chest pain radiating to his left arm, accompanied by nausea.
ECG showed ST-segment elevation and cardiac enzymes were elevated, confirming a myocardial infarction.
He underwent urgent coronary angiography, which revealed a 90% blockage in the left anterior descending artery. He received a stent placement and was started on dual antiplatelet therapy.
Post-procedure, he was educated on lifestyle changes, including smoking cessation, dietary modifications, and regular exercise. He was monitored for potential complications and participated in a cardiac rehabilitation program.
