David, a 62-year-old accountant, had been working in a high-pressure office environment for years, leading to chronic stress and a sedentary lifestyle. He spent long hours at his desk, often skipping meals or consuming high-calorie fast food. Recently, he experienced chest pain and shortness of breath while walking to work, which he initially attributed to stress. However, the symptoms persisted and worsened. Blood tests revealed elevated troponin levels (TnI: 1.5 ng/mL), indicating heart damage, and his lipid panel showed elevated LDL cholesterol (160 mg/dL). His ECG revealed ST-segment depression, and an echocardiogram showed an ejection fraction of 50% with mild hypokinesis in the anterior wall. A coronary angiogram revealed a 70% blockage in his left anterior descending artery (LAD). David underwent PCI with stent placement, and his symptoms improved. He was prescribed statins, aspirin, and beta-blockers. Given his stressful work environment, he was encouraged to manage his stress through mindfulness techniques and to incorporate regular exercise into his daily routine to improve his heart health.
