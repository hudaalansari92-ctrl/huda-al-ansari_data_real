A 58-year-old man with a known high polygenic risk score for coronary artery disease (CAD) presented with recurrent angina despite adhering to his CAD treatment regimen. He had recently retired to a city with high traffic pollution and noticed that his chest pain episodes had increased in frequency since the move. His cardiologist suspected that his genetic susceptibility to CAD, compounded by constant exposure to air pollution, was worsening his condition, potentially accelerating plaque buildup and arterial inflammation. He was advised to consider relocating to an area with lower pollution levels or to take extra precautions, including indoor air purification and avoiding outdoor activities during high pollution periods, to help mitigate further risk.
