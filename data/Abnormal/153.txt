A 30-year-old male named Alex worked in an office located in an area with high levels of air pollution from traffic emissions. He sought medical attention after experiencing episodes of chest discomfort during exercise.
Alex described his chest discomfort as a sharp pain that occurred primarily when he engaged in vigorous activities such as running or playing basketball. He also reported increased fatigue during workouts.
He had no significant chronic diseases but reported frequent respiratory issues, including allergies exacerbated by poor air quality.
His risk factors included exposure to environmental pollutants from heavy traffic near his workplace, a sedentary lifestyle due to long hours at his desk job, and a family history of cardiovascular issues (his grandfather had CAD).
Diagnostic tests revealed significant coronary artery disease. Alex was advised on lifestyle changes, including quitting smoking entirely, improving his diet, and increasing physical activity. He was also referred for occupational health assessments to address environmental exposures at work.
