A 75-year-old female named Helen lived in an area with high levels of air pollution due to nearby factories. She presented with acute chest pain after experiencing increased episodes of shortness of breath during physical activity.
Helen described her chest pain as tightness that worsened during physical exertion or when the air quality was particularly poor. She also experienced fatigue after minimal activity.
Helen had a history of asthma but had been managing it well until recently when she began experiencing more frequent respiratory symptoms associated with pollution exposure.
Her risk factors included long-term exposure to air pollutants from industrial emissions, being overweight (BMI of 29), poorly controlled hypertension, and having a family history of heart disease (her sister suffered from CAD).
After diagnostic testing confirmed significant coronary artery disease alongside acute coronary syndrome linked to thrombosis formation due to vasoconstriction from pollution exposure, Helen underwent coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG). She was started on appropriate medications for both conditions and educated about the importance of reducing exposure to pollutants while adopting healthier lifestyle choices.
