A 70-year-old male named Frank lived in an area known for high levels of air pollution due to nearby highways and factories. He presented with worsening symptoms related to his chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) along with new-onset chest pain.
Frank reported increased shortness of breath while walking short distances and episodes of chest tightness that occurred primarily during outdoor activities or when air quality was poor.
Frank had a long history of COPD due to years of smoking before quitting 15 years ago. His respiratory condition made him more susceptible to the effects of air pollution.
His risk factors included advanced age, long-term exposure to air pollutants (particularly PM2.5), obesity (BMI of 28), and a family history of heart disease (his brother had CAD).
After evaluation revealed significant coronary artery disease alongside worsening COPD symptoms, Frank was started on medications for both conditions. He was advised on strategies to minimize exposure to pollutants, such as staying indoors on days with poor air quality and using air purifiers at home.
