A 77-year-old woman with a history of hypertension, diabetes, and morbid obesity was referred for evaluation after experiencing worsening dyspnea on exertion. Diagnostic imaging revealed significant ischemia and reduced left ventricular ejection fraction, indicating severe CAD.
This patient had previously failed percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) for a chronic total occlusion. Her case illustrates the complexities of treating CAD in patients with severe obesity and multiple cardiovascular risk factors, emphasizing the need for tailored treatment approaches.
