Paul, a 58-year-old factory worker, presented to the emergency room with chest pain and shortness of breath after lifting heavy machinery at work. He had been experiencing occasional chest discomfort for several months but dismissed it as fatigue from his physically demanding job. Paul had a 30-year history of smoking and worked long hours in a noisy, stressful environment with little access to healthy food options. His blood tests revealed elevated troponin levels (TnI: 2.2 ng/mL), confirming heart muscle damage, and his lipid profile showed an LDL cholesterol of 160 mg/dL. His ECG demonstrated ST-segment elevation in the inferior leads, consistent with an acute myocardial infarction. An echocardiogram showed a reduced ejection fraction of 40% with hypokinesis in the inferior wall of the heart. A coronary angiogram revealed a 90% blockage in his right coronary artery (RCA). Paul underwent an urgent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with successful stent placement. Post-procedure, he was placed on aspirin, beta-blockers, and statins and was advised to quit smoking and improve his diet. He was also referred to cardiac rehabilitation to help manage his risk factors, including reducing work-related stress.
