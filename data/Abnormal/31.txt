Mark, a 45-year-old businessman, had a history of high stress, a poor diet, and smoking. His father had suffered a heart attack in his late 40s, but Mark assumed he had time to make lifestyle changes. He led a fast-paced life, frequently travelling for work and relying on fast food and energy drinks to get through long days. Over time, Mark began noticing mild chest discomfort, especially during stressful situations or after eating heavy meals. He brushed it off as indigestion or anxiety. One day, while attending a business meeting, he felt an intense, squeezing pain in his chest, accompanied by shortness of breath and sweating. He was rushed to the hospital, where tests confirmed he was having a heart attack. A coronary angiogram revealed a 90% blockage in his left anterior descending (LAD) artery and an 80% blockage in his right coronary artery. Mark underwent immediate percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI), where stents were placed to open the blocked arteries. After his recovery, Mark's cardiologist emphasized the need for significant lifestyle changes, including quitting smoking, adopting a heart-healthy diet, managing stress, and incorporating regular exercise. Mark successfully quit smoking and made other lifestyle adjustments, which, along with his medication regimen, helped lower his risk of future cardiac events.
