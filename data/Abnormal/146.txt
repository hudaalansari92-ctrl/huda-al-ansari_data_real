A 58-year-old female named Linda with rheumatoid arthritis lived in an area with high levels of air pollution due to nearby factories. She presented with increased joint pain and reported new-onset chest discomfort during physical activity.
Linda described her chest discomfort as a tightness that occurred mainly during flare-ups of her arthritis or on days when the air quality was particularly poor. She also reported increased fatigue and difficulty breathing during outdoor activities.
Linda's chronic inflammatory condition (rheumatoid arthritis) puts her at higher risk for cardiovascular issues due to systemic inflammation.
Living near industrial areas exposed her to elevated levels of particulate matter (PM2.5) and other pollutants. Additionally, after comprehensive evaluations indicated early signs of coronary artery disease, Linda was started on medications for both her arthritis and cardiovascular health. She was educated about the importance of managing inflammation and reducing exposure to environmental pollutants.
