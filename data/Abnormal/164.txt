A 60-year-old man with a history of coronary artery disease (CAD) and prior stent placement presented to the emergency department with sudden chest pain and sweating after working outdoors in a highly polluted urban area. He had previously managed his CAD with medication, but his symptoms worsened over the last few months. His cardiologist suspected that long-term exposure to air pollution had promoted atherosclerosis and increased plaque instability, contributing to the likelihood of plaque rupture. An angiogram confirmed a new blockage caused by a ruptured plaque, leading to a myocardial infarction. Following treatment, he was advised to avoid outdoor activities in high-pollution areas and to monitor his heart health regularly.
