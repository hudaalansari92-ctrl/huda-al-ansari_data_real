David, a 60-year-old man with hereditary hemochromatosis, had been treated for years with regular phlebotomy to manage the iron overload in his body. Recently, he began experiencing episodes of chest pain and shortness of breath, especially after exertion. Hemochromatosis, a condition where excess iron builds up in organs, can cause damage to the heart and increase the risk of coronary artery disease (CAD). Blood tests showed elevated troponin levels, and his ECG revealed signs of ischemia. An echocardiogram demonstrated reduced heart function with an ejection fraction of 45%, and a coronary angiogram revealed significant blockages, including a 90% blockage in the right coronary artery. David was diagnosed with CAD and underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. Post-procedure, his symptoms improved, and he was started on statins and aspirin while continuing treatment for his hemochromatosis to prevent further complications.
