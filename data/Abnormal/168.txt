A 65-year-old man with a known history of coronary artery disease (CAD) and previous stent placements reported an episode of acute chest pain and lightheadedness while on vacation in a large city with high air pollution levels. Although he had been stable on his CAD medications, his doctors found that exposure to airborne pollutants likely enhanced coagulation and thrombosis within his coronary arteries, contributing to clot formation. Further investigation revealed a small clot near one of his stents, which was treated with anticoagulant therapy. His cardiologist emphasized the importance of reducing pollution exposure, particularly in areas known for high particulate matter, to lower his risk of future thrombotic events.
