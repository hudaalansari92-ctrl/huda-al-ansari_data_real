Sarah is a 62-year-old retired school teacher living in a densely populated urban area with high levels of air pollution due to traffic and nearby industrial activities. With a family history of heart disease, including her father suffering a heart attack at 65, Sarah has been managing hypertension for the past decade and has mildly elevated cholesterol levels. After experiencing recurrent chest pain and shortness of breath during her daily walks, she underwent genetic testing that revealed a high polygenic risk score for coronary artery disease (CAD). Following a stress test and coronary angiography, it was confirmed that she had 70% stenosis in the left anterior descending artery. Her management plan includes statins and beta-blockers, lifestyle modifications such as a heart-healthy diet, and avoiding outdoor activities on high-pollution days.
