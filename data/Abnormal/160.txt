A 60-year-old woman with a history of coronary artery disease (CAD) and a recent genetic test revealing a high polygenic risk score for cardiovascular conditions began noticing increased chest tightness and fatigue. Although her CAD had been managed with medication, her symptoms worsened after she moved to a highly industrialized area with frequent air quality advisories. Her cardiologist attributed her increasing symptoms to the interaction between her genetic predisposition and high levels of air pollution, which heightened her vulnerability to ischemic events. She was advised to avoid outdoor activities on high-pollution days, use a quality air filtration system indoors, and schedule frequent check-ins to monitor her CAD progression.
