Sarah, a 49-year-old office worker, had been leading a sedentary lifestyle for years, working long hours behind a desk with minimal physical activity. She was also a smoker for over 20 years, which further compounded her health risks. Recently, she began experiencing chest tightness and fatigue during simple activities, such as walking to her office from the parking lot. Blood tests revealed elevated troponin levels (TnI: 0.9 ng/mL), and her lipid profile showed an LDL cholesterol of 180 mg/dL and total cholesterol of 240 mg/dL. Her ECG revealed non-specific T-wave inversions, and an echocardiogram showed an ejection fraction of 55% with mild hypokinesis in the inferior wall. A coronary angiogram revealed a 70% blockage in her right coronary artery. Sarah underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. After the procedure, her chest pain improved. She was prescribed statins, aspirin, and beta-blockers and was enrolled in a smoking cessation program. Given her sedentary work environment, she was encouraged to incorporate regular physical activity into her daily routine to reduce her risk of further coronary events.
