A 32-year-old female named Emily worked in an office located in a highly polluted urban area. She sought medical attention after experiencing episodes of chest discomfort and palpitations during her commute.
Emily described her chest discomfort as a tightness that occurred primarily during her daily commute through heavy traffic. She also reported increased anxiety and fatigue, especially after long days at work.
She had no significant chronic diseases but had experienced asthma exacerbations linked to poor air quality.
Her risk factors included exposure to high levels of air pollution from vehicle emissions, a sedentary lifestyle due to long hours at her desk job, and a family history of cardiovascular issues (her mother had CAD).
After diagnostic testing confirmed early signs of coronary artery disease, Emily was advised on lifestyle modifications, including quitting smoking, improving her diet, and increasing physical activity. She was placed on medications, including statins, for cholesterol management and was educated about the importance of reducing exposure to pollutants.
