A 52-year-old man with a long history of psoriatic arthritis, an autoimmune condition that causes chronic joint inflammation, visited the emergency department with chest pain and shortness of breath. In addition to his psoriatic arthritis, he had several traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD): he was a smoker, had high cholesterol, and had a sedentary lifestyle due to joint pain from his condition. Concerned, his physician ordered an ECG and coronary angiography, which revealed significant CAD with blockages in two major arteries, requiring stent placement. His cardiologist explained that the combination of traditional risk factors like smoking, high cholesterol, and inactivity, along with the chronic inflammation from psoriatic arthritis, likely accelerated plaque buildup in his arteries. Following the procedure, he was advised to quit smoking, manage his arthritis to stay active, adopt a heart-healthy diet, and schedule regular follow-ups to monitor his cardiovascular health.
