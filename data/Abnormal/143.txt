A 62-year-old male named Tom lived in an area with high levels of air pollution due to nearby factories. He presented with symptoms consistent with angina after experiencing increased episodes of chest pain while walking his dog.
Tom described the chest pain as pressure-like discomfort that worsened during physical activity. He also experienced shortness of breath when climbing stairs or walking briskly.
His risk factors included long-term exposure to air pollution from industrial emissions, being overweight (BMI of 28), smoking history (quit smoking five years ago), and having high blood pressure that was not well-controlled.
After diagnostic testing confirmed significant coronary artery disease, Tom was placed on appropriate medications to manage his condition. He was advised on the importance of reducing pollution exposure and encouraged to adopt healthier lifestyle choices.
