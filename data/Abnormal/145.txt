A 30-year-old male named Alex worked in a manufacturing plant located in an industrial zone known for high levels of air pollution. He sought medical attention after experiencing persistent chest discomfort and episodes of dizziness.
Alex reported that the chest discomfort felt like pressure and often occurred after long shifts at work. He also experienced palpitations and shortness of breath during physical exertion.
He had no significant chronic diseases but reported frequent respiratory issues, including bronchitis, which he attributed to the poor air quality at his workplace.
His risk factors included exposure to industrial pollutants, a sedentary lifestyle due to long hours at his desk job, and a family history of cardiovascular issues (his grandfather had CAD). He also admitted to smoking socially during college but quit five years ago.
Diagnostic tests revealed significant coronary artery disease. Alex was advised on lifestyle changes, including quitting smoking entirely, improving his diet, and increasing physical activity. He was also referred for occupational health assessments to address environmental exposures at work.
