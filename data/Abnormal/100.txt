A 37-year-old female patient with a diagnosis of Crohn's disease presented with chest discomfort and was subsequently diagnosed with coronary artery disease (CAD). Despite her young age and the absence of traditional cardiovascular risk factors such as hypertension, diabetes, or hyperlipidemia, her chronic inflammatory condition significantly contributed to her cardiovascular risk. The patient had a history of frequent flare-ups of Crohn's disease, which led to persistent systemic inflammation characterized by elevated levels of C-reactive protein (CRP) and other inflammatory markers. Additionally, she experienced episodes of anxiety and stress related to her illness, which further exacerbated her condition. Imaging studies revealed significant coronary arterial plaque formation, indicating accelerated atherosclerosis.
