Ali, a 57-year-old construction worker, has a strong family history of heart disease, as his father passed away from a heart attack and his mother suffers from diabetes. For 35 years, he was a heavy smoker, consuming two packs of cigarettes daily. He also suffers from obesity (BMI 32), high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, and elevated levels of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol and triglycerides. He began experiencing chest pain while working, especially during physical exertion, accompanied by fatigue, shortness of breath, and dizziness. After experiencing a severe chest pain episode lasting more than 15 minutes, he underwent medical tests that revealed myocardial ischemia, elevated cardiac enzymes, and a 70% narrowing in the left anterior descending (LAD) coronary artery and a 50% narrowing in the right coronary artery (RCA), leading to a diagnosis of coronary artery disease (CAD) and a minor heart attack. A treatment plan was implemented, including smoking cessation through a support program, adopting a healthy diet under a nutritionist’s supervision, and engaging in regular physical exercise. Additionally, he was prescribed medications to lower cholesterol and blood pressure and manage diabetes, along with undergoing a therapeutic catheterization with stent placement in the narrowed artery. After six months of treatment and lifestyle changes, his condition significantly improved—his cholesterol and blood pressure levels decreased, his ability to perform daily activities improved, and he completely quit smoking while also losing some excess weight. Ali continues regular medical follow-ups, adhering to a healthy lifestyle and preventive medications to reduce the risk of future complications.
