Sarah, a 64-year-old high school teacher, had spent over 30 years in the education system, often working long hours grading papers, preparing lessons, and managing student behavior. As she approached retirement, Sarah began feeling increased pressure in her chest during the school day, particularly when walking between classrooms or dealing with stressful situations. The tightness in her chest was accompanied by occasional dizziness and fatigue. Because she loved her job, Sarah initially attributed her symptoms to the stress of managing her students and meeting tight deadlines. After a particularly stressful parent-teacher conference left her feeling lightheaded and breathless, she sought medical care. Given her high-stress occupation, age, and symptoms, Sarah's doctor suspected coronary artery disease (CAD). An evaluation confirmed that she had multiple blockages in her coronary arteries. Sarah underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. After the procedure, she reduced her workload and incorporated regular physical activity into her routine, which helped improve her heart health and overall well-being.
