A 42-year-old female named Lisa worked on a rotating shift schedule as a nurse. She came to the clinic complaining of persistent fatigue and occasional episodes of chest pain after long shifts.
Lisa reported that her chest pain felt like pressure in the center of her chest and occurred mainly after night shifts. She also experienced shortness of breath during physical activity.
Her risk factors included her shift work schedule, which disrupted her sleep patterns, leading to chronic fatigue. She had a history of smoking (quit two years ago) and irregular eating habits due to her unpredictable work hours.
Diagnostic tests revealed significant coronary artery disease. Lisa underwent an angioplasty to open blocked arteries and was counseled on the importance of regular sleep patterns, nutrition, and exercise in managing her heart health.
