A 58-year-old female with a history of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) presented for evaluation due to fatigue and chest discomfort. She had been managing her condition with immunosuppressive therapy but expressed concerns about her cardiovascular health due to her chronic inflammatory disease. Given her history, she underwent a cardiac evaluation that included an ECG, echocardiogram, and coronary angiography. The results indicated significant coronary artery disease, including lesions in multiple vessels. The patient was started on aggressive medical management for her CAD, including antiplatelet therapy and statins. She continued her treatment for SLE while being monitored closely for cardiovascular events.
