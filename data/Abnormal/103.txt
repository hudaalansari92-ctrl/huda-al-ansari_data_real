A 49-year-old man with a long-standing history of Crohn's disease, a chronic inflammatory bowel disease, sought medical advice after experiencing recurring chest discomfort and unusual fatigue during daily activities. Although he had no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), he was a non-smoker, had normal blood pressure and healthy cholesterol levels, and maintained a normal body weight, and his Crohn's disease had been active over many years, leading to frequent inflammatory episodes. An evaluation, including an ECG and coronary angiography, revealed a significant coronary blockage requiring stent placement. His cardiologist explained that the chronic inflammation associated with Crohn's disease likely accelerated the progression of atherosclerosis in his coronary arteries. Post-procedure, he was advised to maintain strict control of his Crohn's symptoms, adopt an anti-inflammatory diet, and schedule regular cardiovascular follow-ups to reduce future heart health risks.
