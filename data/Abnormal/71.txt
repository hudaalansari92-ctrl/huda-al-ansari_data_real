Karen, a 55-year-old woman with a history of hyperthyroidism, had been experiencing palpitations, chest pain, and episodes of dizziness for several months. Her thyroid levels had been poorly controlled, and she was already on anti-thyroid medication. As her symptoms worsened, her doctor ordered further tests to evaluate her heart. Blood tests revealed elevated troponin levels, suggesting heart damage, and her ECG showed non-specific T-wave changes. An echocardiogram revealed reduced heart function with an ejection fraction of 50%, and a coronary angiogram confirmed significant blockages, including an 85% blockage in the left circumflex artery. Karen was diagnosed with coronary artery disease (CAD), likely worsened by the effects of hyperthyroidism on her cardiovascular system. She underwent a successful PCI with stent placement and was started on statins and beta-blockers in addition to adjusting her treatment for hyperthyroidism.
