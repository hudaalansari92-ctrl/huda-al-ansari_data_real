A 58-year-old female presented for evaluation after experiencing episodes of chest pain that occurred during physical activity. She had been diagnosed with hyperlipidemia three years prior, with total cholesterol levels consistently above 240 mg/dL despite being on statin therapy.
The patient described her chest pain as a pressure-like sensation that radiated to her left arm and jaw. She also reported fatigue and occasional shortness of breath during exertion.
Her risk factors included a family history of premature coronary artery disease (her father had a heart attack at age 50), a sedentary lifestyle, and a high-stress job as a financial analyst.
An angiogram revealed significant blockages in multiple coronary arteries. The patient underwent coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG) and was educated about the importance of dietary modifications and regular exercise to manage her hyperlipidemia and reduce the risk of further cardiovascular events.
