A 62-year-old man with a known history of coronary artery disease (CAD) presented to the emergency department after experiencing chest pain and shortness of breath while walking near a busy industrial area. He had a history of hypertension and hypercholesterolemia, which were managed with medication. The patient reported that his symptoms worsened during times when he was exposed to heavy traffic and poor air quality. An ECG and troponin tests indicated myocardial ischemia. His cardiologist explained that acute arterial vasoconstriction triggered by air pollution exposure likely reduced blood flow to his already compromised coronary arteries, contributing to his chest pain and ischemic episode. He was advised to limit exposure to high-pollution areas and was provided with medication adjustments to manage his CAD under these conditions better.
