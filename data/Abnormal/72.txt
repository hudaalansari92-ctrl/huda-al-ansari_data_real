George, a 66-year-old man with severe psoriasis for over 20 years, started experiencing chest pain and fatigue during physical activity. Psoriasis, a chronic inflammatory skin condition, is known to increase the risk of atherosclerosis due to the systemic inflammation it causes. Concerned about the possibility of coronary artery disease (CAD), his doctor ordered tests to assess his heart health. Blood tests revealed elevated troponin levels, indicating cardiac damage, and his ECG showed ST-segment depression. An echocardiogram revealed mild hypokinesis with an ejection fraction of 45%. A coronary angiogram identified significant blockages, including a 75% blockage in the left anterior descending (LAD) artery. George was diagnosed with CAD and underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. After the procedure, his chest pain improved, and he was placed on statins, aspirin, and anti-inflammatory medications to manage both his heart disease and psoriasis.
