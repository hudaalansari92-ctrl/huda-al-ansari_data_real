A 50-year-old male named Tom worked as an executive in a high-stress corporate environment located in a city with significant air pollution issues. He came in for evaluation due to worsening chest pain and anxiety.
 Tom reported experiencing chest pain that felt like pressure during stressful meetings or after long workdays. He also noted increased heart rate and difficulty sleeping due to stress.
While he did not have any diagnosed chronic diseases, he had elevated blood pressure readings during previous visits that he attributed to stress.
His risk factors included high levels of occupational stress, long working hours that promoted a sedentary lifestyle, poor dietary habits (frequent takeout meals), and exposure to outdoor pollutants from heavy traffic near his office.
After diagnostic testing confirmed early-stage coronary artery disease, Tom was placed on medication for hypertension and cholesterol management. He was advised on stress management techniques and encouraged to make healthier lifestyle choices.
