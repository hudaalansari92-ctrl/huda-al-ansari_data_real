Anna, a 64-year-old woman living with Sjogren's syndrome, a chronic autoimmune condition that primarily affects the body's moisture-producing glands, began experiencing persistent chest pain and extreme fatigue. Sjogren's syndrome is associated with chronic inflammation, increasing the risk of cardiovascular diseases. Concerned about her symptoms, Anna's doctor conducted blood tests, which revealed elevated troponin levels, and an ECG showed ST-segment depression, indicating ischemia. An echocardiogram confirmed reduced heart function, with an ejection fraction of 50%. A coronary angiogram revealed an 85% blockage in the left anterior descending (LAD) artery. Anna was diagnosed with coronary artery disease (CAD) and underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. She was placed on statins and aspirin to manage her CAD while continuing her immunosuppressive treatment for Sjogren's syndrome. Her symptoms improved significantly following the procedure.
