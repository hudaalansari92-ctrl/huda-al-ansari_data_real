Carlos, a 63-year-old chef, worked in a busy restaurant kitchen where he faced high levels of stress due to the fast-paced environment and long hours. He often worked late into the night, standing for extended periods, preparing meals under pressure, and rarely taking breaks to eat healthy food himself. His meals mostly consisted of quick snacks or heavy, rich foods he prepared at work. Over time, Carlos began experiencing chest pain and shortness of breath during his shifts, especially during peak service hours. The chest pain, which felt like a tightening sensation, became more frequent, and he found himself unable to keep up with the physical demands of his job. Worried about his symptoms, Carlos finally sought medical attention. His doctor, concerned about his high-stress work environment and poor diet, suspected coronary artery disease (CAD). After evaluation, significant blockages were found in his coronary arteries. Carlos underwent coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG) and was advised to modify his diet, manage stress levels, and adopt a healthier lifestyle. He also adjusted his work schedule to reduce the physical and mental strain from his job.
