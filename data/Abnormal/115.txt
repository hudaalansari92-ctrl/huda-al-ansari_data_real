A 47-year-old man with a history of ankylosing spondylitis (AS), a chronic autoimmune disease that causes inflammation in the spine and other joints, visited the emergency department with symptoms of chest pain and difficulty breathing, especially during mild physical activities. Despite having no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), he was a non-smoker, had normal cholesterol levels, and maintained a healthy weight, and his ankylosing spondylitis had caused ongoing inflammation in his body for many years. Given his symptoms, his doctor ordered an ECG and coronary angiography, which revealed a significant blockage in one of his coronary arteries, confirming CAD and necessitating stent placement. His cardiologist explained that the chronic systemic inflammation from AS likely contributed to his CAD, accelerating plaque buildup in his arteries despite the absence of other risk factors. Post-procedure, he was advised to continue managing his autoimmune condition carefully, adopt an anti-inflammatory diet, and undergo regular follow-ups to monitor his cardiovascular health and prevent further issues.
