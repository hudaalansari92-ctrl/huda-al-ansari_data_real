A 55-year-old female named Linda, with a history of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), came in for her routine follow-up. She expressed concerns about her cardiovascular health due to the inflammatory nature of her condition.
Linda reported persistent fatigue, joint pain, and episodes of chest tightness that occurred during periods of increased activity or stress. She also experienced palpitations occasionally but no significant chest pain.
Her risk factors included chronic inflammation due to SLE, prolonged use of corticosteroids for treatment, obesity (BMI of 30), and a family history of heart disease (her mother had CAD).
After comprehensive evaluations indicated early signs of coronary artery disease, Linda was started on aggressive management for both her lupus and cardiovascular health. She was educated about the importance of controlling inflammation and maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
