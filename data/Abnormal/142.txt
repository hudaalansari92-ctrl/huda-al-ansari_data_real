A 55-year-old female named Sarah worked as a nurse in an emergency department. She presented with complaints of chest pain that she attributed to stress from her job.
Sarah described her chest pain as sharp and occurring primarily during high-stress shifts. She also reported feeling fatigued and anxious, especially after particularly busy days at work.
Her risk factors included high levels of occupational stress, irregular eating habits due to long shifts, lack of physical activity because of her demanding job, and a family history of heart disease (her grandmother had CAD).
After evaluation revealed significant coronary artery disease, Sarah was started on medications for her cholesterol and blood pressure. She was also referred to stress management programs and encouraged to exercise regularly.
