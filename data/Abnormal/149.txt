A 65-year-old male named Frank lived in a heavily industrialized city with high levels of air pollution. He presented to the clinic with complaints of chest pain and shortness of breath, especially when walking outside.
Frank described his chest pain as a tightness that occurred primarily during physical activity. He also reported persistent coughing and fatigue, particularly on days when air quality was poor.
He had a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and hypertension. His respiratory condition made him more susceptible to the effects of air pollution.
His risk factors included long-term exposure to air pollutants such as particulate matter (PM2.5), a sedentary lifestyle, and a family history of heart disease (his brother had CAD). Frank had also been a smoker for 20 years before quitting five years ago.
After diagnostic testing revealed significant coronary artery disease, Frank was started on medications for both his CAD and COPD. He was advised to limit outdoor activities on days with poor air quality and encouraged to consider relocating to a less polluted area for better health outcomes.
