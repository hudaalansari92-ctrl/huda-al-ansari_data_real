An 88-year-old female with a history of chronic coronary artery disease underwent multiple interventions over the years, including successful angioplasty and stent placements. Despite these interventions, she continued to experience angina and was diagnosed with in-stent restenosis and new obstructions in her coronary arteries.
Her management included optimizing her medication regimen, which involved statins and other lipid-lowering agents, to control her cholesterol levels.
