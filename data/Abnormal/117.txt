A 50-year-old man with a long-standing history of psoriasis, an autoimmune skin condition known for causing chronic inflammation, visited his doctor with symptoms of chest tightness and shortness of breath during moderate activity. Despite lacking traditional coronary artery disease (CAD) risk factors, he was a non-smoker, had normal blood pressure and cholesterol, and maintained a healthy weight. His psoriasis had been active for over a decade, with frequent flare-ups leading to persistent systemic inflammation. Concerned about his symptoms, his physician ordered an ECG and coronary angiography, which revealed significant CAD with a 70% blockage in one of his coronary arteries, requiring stent placement. His cardiologist explained that the chronic inflammation from psoriasis likely accelerated plaque buildup in his arteries, increasing his risk for CAD even without other risk factors. Following the procedure, he was advised to continue managing his psoriasis closely, incorporate anti-inflammatory foods into his diet, and undergo regular cardiovascular monitoring to prevent future complications.
