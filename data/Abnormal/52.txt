Maria, a 55-year-old woman working long, physically demanding shifts in a factory, experienced ongoing fatigue and occasional chest discomfort. Her work environment, which involved extended hours and limited breaks, left her with little time for exercise or healthy eating. She relied on quick, processed meals during her shifts. Over time, the chest discomfort became more frequent and severe, especially when climbing stairs at work. At the hospital, blood tests showed elevated troponin levels (TnI: 1.8 ng/mL), and her lipid profile revealed high cholesterol, with LDL at 170 mg/dL. Her ECG revealed ST-segment depression, and an echocardiogram showed a reduced ejection fraction of 45% with hypokinesis in the anterior wall. A coronary angiogram found a 75% blockage in the left circumflex artery. Maria underwent PCI with stent placement and was started on aspirin, statins, and beta-blockers. Post-recovery, she was counseled on improving her diet and incorporating more physical activity into her daily routine despite the demands of her work schedule.
