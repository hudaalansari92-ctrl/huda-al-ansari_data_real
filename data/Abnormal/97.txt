A 47-year-old woman with a long-standing diagnosis of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) presented to the emergency department with chest pain and fatigue. Despite having no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), she was a non-smoker, had normal blood pressure and cholesterol levels, and maintained a healthy body weight, and she had struggled with chronic inflammation and intermittent flare-ups from lupus for over 20 years. Given her symptoms, doctors conducted an ECG and coronary angiography, which revealed significant CAD with a 60% blockage in one of her coronary arteries, requiring stent placement. Her cardiologist explained that the chronic systemic inflammation associated with SLE likely contributed to early atherosclerosis, inflaming her blood vessels and increasing cardiovascular risk despite the absence of traditional factors. She was advised to continue aggressive management of her lupus, incorporate a heart-healthy diet, and monitor her cardiovascular health regularly to minimize future complications.
