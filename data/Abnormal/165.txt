A 58-year-old woman with known coronary artery disease (CAD) and a family history of heart disease experienced recurring chest pain and fatigue while living in an industrial city with persistently high air pollution levels. Despite medication adherence, her chest pain became more frequent, and she experienced a heart attack after a particularly intense week of pollution exposure. Her cardiologist explained that chronic exposure to pollutants likely promoted the progression and destabilization of atherosclerosis plaques, increasing her risk of acute coronary events. She was advised to relocate to an area with cleaner air if possible and was provided with additional medication to help manage her CAD.
