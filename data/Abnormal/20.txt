David, a 42-year-old office worker living in a bustling city, leads a high-pressure lifestyle. His job in a corporate office requires him to meet tight deadlines, manage multiple projects, and constantly be available for phone calls and emails, which often leads to long working hours. On top of this, his daily commute takes over an hour in heavy traffic, adding to his stress. To cope, David often skips meals during the day and then overeats fast food at night. His work is mostly sedentary, as he spends the majority of his time sitting at a desk, with little time or energy left for exercise. Over the years, the combination of chronic stress, poor diet, and lack of physical activity causes David to gain weight, eventually leading to obesity. His blood pressure steadily rises, but he ignores the warning signs, attributing them to work-related fatigue. Eventually, he is diagnosed with hypertension, but he continues his unhealthy habits due to the constant demands of his job. One day, after feeling unusually short of breath and experiencing chest pain, David visits his doctor and learns that he is at high risk for coronary artery disease (CAD). His chronic stress, obesity, and uncontrolled hypertension have all contributed to the narrowing of his arteries, putting him at risk for a heart attack.
