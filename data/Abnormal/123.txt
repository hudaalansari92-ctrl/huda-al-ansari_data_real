A 60-year-old female with a history of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) presented for evaluation due to fatigue and chest discomfort that had become more pronounced over the past few months. She had been managing her SLE with corticosteroids but expressed concerns about her cardiovascular health.
Her risk factors included chronic inflammation due to SLE, prolonged corticosteroid use, high cholesterol levels, and a family history of heart disease.
Given her symptoms and history, she underwent an echocardiogram, which indicated reduced left ventricular function. Coronary angiography revealed significant lesions in multiple vessels consistent with CAD.
The patient was started on aggressive medical management for her CAD, including antiplatelet therapy and statins. She continued her treatment for SLE while being monitored closely for cardiovascular events.
