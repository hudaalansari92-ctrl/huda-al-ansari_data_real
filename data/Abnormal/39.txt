Susan, a 72-year-old retired schoolteacher with a history of hypertension, presented for a routine check-up and reported mild fatigue and occasional dizziness but no chest pain. Her ECG showed nonspecific changes, and her blood tests revealed mildly elevated troponin and LDL cholesterol. Despite her lack of classic symptoms, an echocardiogram showed reduced ejection fraction (45%) and mild hypokinesis of the left ventricle. A stress test revealed ischemia, prompting further investigation. Coronary angiography revealed an 85% blockage in her left circumflex artery and a 70% blockage in her right coronary artery. Susan was diagnosed with silent coronary artery disease (CAD) and underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. She was placed on statins, beta-blockers, and aspirin, and her fatigue resolved after the procedure. Follow-up echocardiography showed modest improvement in her heart function, and her troponin levels normalized.
