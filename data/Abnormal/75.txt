Sarah, a 63-year-old woman who had been diagnosed with celiac disease 20 years ago, developed chest pain and fatigue over several months. Although she adhered to a gluten-free diet, she had a history of malabsorption issues and nutritional deficiencies, which are common in celiac disease. Blood tests showed elevated troponin levels, indicating heart damage, and her lipid profile revealed high cholesterol levels, likely due to malabsorption-related inflammation. Her ECG showed ischemic changes, and an echocardiogram demonstrated reduced heart function with an ejection fraction of 50%. A coronary angiogram revealed an 80% blockage in her right coronary artery. Sarah was diagnosed with coronary artery disease (CAD) and underwent successful percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. Post-procedure, she was started on statins, aspirin, and nutritional supplements to address deficiencies, and her heart health improved.
