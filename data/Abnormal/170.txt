A 68-year-old male named Richard lived in a city with high levels of air pollution due to industrial emissions. He presented to the emergency department with severe chest pain and shortness of breath after a particularly smoggy day.
Richard described his chest pain as a heavy pressure that worsened during physical activity. He also reported feeling dizzy and fatigued, particularly after exercise.
He had a history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes, both of which were poorly controlled. Richard also had chronic bronchitis, which he attributed to years of exposure to polluted air.
His risk factors included long-term exposure to particulate matter (PM2.5) and other pollutants from living near factories and heavy traffic. He had a family history of heart disease; his father had suffered a heart attack at age 60.
Diagnostic tests revealed significant coronary artery disease with evidence of plaque rupture and acute thrombosis in one of the coronary arteries. Richard underwent angioplasty with stent placement and was started on dual antiplatelet therapy (aspirin and clopidogrel). He was advised to limit outdoor activities on days with poor air quality and to adopt lifestyle changes, including dietary modifications.
