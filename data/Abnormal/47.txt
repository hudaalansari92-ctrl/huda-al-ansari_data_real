Mary, a 65-year-old retired farmer, came to her primary care doctor complaining of worsening chest tightness and shortness of breath, particularly after doing light farm work like feeding animals. She had been living in a rural area with limited access to healthcare and had managed her high blood pressure inconsistently. Due to the remote location, she had difficulty maintaining a healthy diet, often relying on processed and high-sodium foods. Her blood tests showed mildly elevated troponin levels (TnI: 0.7 ng/mL) and high LDL cholesterol (190 mg/dL). Her ECG showed non-specific ST-T wave changes, while her echocardiogram revealed an ejection fraction of 50%, with mild hypokinesis in the anterior wall. A coronary angiogram showed multiple blockages, including a 75% blockage in the left anterior descending (LAD) artery. Mary was diagnosed with chronic coronary artery disease and underwent a percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stenting. After treatment, she was prescribed medications, including statins and blood pressure drugs, and was advised to improve her diet and engage in light physical activity within her rural lifestyle.
