A 60-year-old male named Robert, who lived in a highly industrialized urban area, presented to the clinic with complaints of chest pain and shortness of breath during physical exertion. He lived in this area for over 30 years, surrounded by factories and heavy traffic.
Robert reported experiencing angina-like symptoms, including tightness in his chest and difficulty breathing when walking uphill or engaging in physical activities. He also noted increased fatigue and occasional dizziness.
His risk factors included long-term exposure to air pollution, a sedentary lifestyle due to a desk job, and a family history of heart disease (his father had a heart attack at age 55). He was also a former smoker, having quit five years prior.
After diagnostic tests confirmed significant coronary artery disease, Robert was started on medication to manage his cholesterol and blood pressure. He was advised to relocate to a less polluted environment if possible and encouraged to adopt a healthier lifestyle with regular exercise.
