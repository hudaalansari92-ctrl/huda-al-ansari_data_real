Michael a 60-year-old retired firefighter who has lived in an urban area known for its poor air quality. He has a history of hypertension and high cholesterol, both of which are being managed with medication. Recently, Michael noticed an increase in episodes of chest pain and fatigue. After experiencing a particularly intense episode while working in his garden on a smoggy day, he sought medical attention. Diagnostic tests revealed that he was experiencing acute arterial vasoconstriction, which had contributed to myocardial ischemia. Given his long-term exposure to air pollution and existing cardiovascular risk factors, his cardiologist emphasized the need for lifestyle modifications, including regular exercise, dietary changes, and strict adherence to his medication regimen to mitigate the risk of coronary artery disease. Michael was also advised to monitor air quality reports and limit outdoor activities during high pollution periods.
