A 60-year-old man with a 10-year history of coronary artery disease (CAD) and type 2 diabetes was admitted after a mild heart attack. The patient had been experiencing chest pain more frequently while working outdoors in an area with elevated air pollution levels. Diagnostic tests confirmed myocardial ischemia and moderate plaque buildup, with coronary vasospasms observed on angiography. His cardiologist concluded that airborne pollutants likely triggered vasoconstriction in his coronary arteries, restricting blood flow and contributing to his heart attack. Following treatment, he was advised to reduce outdoor activity during peak pollution hours, manage his diabetes aggressively, and continue close monitoring of his cardiovascular health to prevent further incidents.
