A 57-year-old woman with a history of polymyositis, a rare autoimmune disease causing chronic inflammation and muscle weakness, presented to her physician with symptoms of chest pain and fatigue, particularly when performing light household tasks. She had no traditional coronary artery disease (CAD) risk factors, her blood pressure and cholesterol levels were normal, she did not smoke, and she maintained a healthy weight. However, the persistent systemic inflammation from her polymyositis had affected her health over the years. Concerned by her symptoms, her doctor ordered an ECG and coronary angiography, which revealed significant CAD with a 70% blockage in one of her coronary arteries, requiring a stent. Her cardiologist explained that the chronic inflammation from polymyositis likely contributed to the development of CAD, accelerating plaque buildup even without traditional risk factors. Following the procedure, she was advised to continue managing her autoimmune condition closely, adopt an anti-inflammatory diet, and attend regular cardiovascular check-ups to reduce the risk of further complications.
