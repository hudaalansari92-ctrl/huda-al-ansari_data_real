A 62-year-old woman with a 20-year history of psoriatic arthritis presented to her cardiologist after experiencing mild chest pain and shortness of breath while climbing stairs. Despite having no traditional coronary artery disease (CAD) risk factors, she was a non-smoker, maintained healthy cholesterol levels, and was within a normal weight range, and her psoriatic arthritis had led to chronic, systemic inflammation, which required periodic treatment adjustments. A stress test and subsequent coronary angiogram revealed a significant blockage in one of her coronary arteries, confirming CAD that necessitated stent placement. Her cardiologist explained that the persistent inflammation from psoriatic arthritis likely contributed to her cardiovascular disease, accelerating atherosclerosis over time. Following the procedure, she was advised to continue aggressive management of her psoriatic arthritis, incorporate anti-inflammatory foods into her diet, and follow a regular cardiovascular screening schedule to reduce the risk of future heart-related complications.
