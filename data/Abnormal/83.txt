Paul, a 65-year-old man with type 2 diabetes, had been experiencing frequent chest pain for several months. The pain, described as a heavy, pressure-like sensation, often occurred when he engaged in activities like walking or doing household chores. Additionally, Paul noticed that he felt unusually tired and out of breath after minimal exertion. His diabetes had been poorly controlled for years, and his doctor had previously warned him about the increased risk of heart disease. Given his symptoms and long history of diabetes, coronary artery disease (CAD) was suspected. After further evaluation, it was confirmed that Paul had significant coronary blockages. He underwent coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG) surgery to restore adequate blood flow to his heart. Following the surgery, Paul's symptoms significantly improved, and he was placed on medications to manage his diabetes and prevent further cardiovascular complications. He was also encouraged to follow a strict diet and exercise plan to improve his overall health.
