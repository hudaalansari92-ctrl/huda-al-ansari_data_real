Paul, a 65-year-old farmer living in a rural area, had poorly controlled diabetes and limited access to healthcare facilities. His demanding work schedule and lack of nearby medical resources made it difficult for him to seek regular care. Over time, he began noticing chest pain and shortness of breath, especially after working in the fields. He eventually sought medical attention after experiencing more severe chest discomfort while lifting heavy equipment. Blood tests revealed significantly elevated troponin levels (TnI: 2.5 ng/mL), indicating substantial heart damage. His lipid profile showed an LDL cholesterol of 190 mg/dL, and his HbA1c was 9.5%, indicating poorly controlled diabetes. His ECG showed ST-segment elevation in the inferior leads, consistent with an acute myocardial infarction (heart attack). An echocardiogram showed an ejection fraction of 35% with akinesis in the inferior wall. A coronary angiogram revealed a 90% blockage in the right coronary artery, and Paul underwent emergency percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. Post-procedure, his chest pain resolved, and he was placed on medications to manage his diabetes, cholesterol, and blood pressure. He was advised to follow up closely with his healthcare provider despite the challenges of living in a remote area.
