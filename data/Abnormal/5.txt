A 66-year-old male presented with severe chest pain, breathlessness, sweating, and cough, alongside numbness in his left foot. His medical history included type 2 diabetes and hypertension, with a significant family history of CAD, both parents had the disease, and his father died from it.
He was a car driver and smoked 5-8 cigarettes daily for the past 10 years. His diet was rich in meat, seafood, and pickles.
He had a foot ulcer due to diabetes, which was complicated by gangrene. His cardiovascular examination revealed elevated blood pressure (150/90 mmHg) and a normal heart rate.
The treatment plan included lifestyle modifications, medication adherence (antidiabetics, antihypertensives, and statins), and regular follow-ups. The emphasis was on controlling blood sugar levels and managing lipid profiles to mitigate CAD progression.
