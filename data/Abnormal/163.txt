A 62-year-old woman with known CAD and a previous stent placement started experiencing increased chest tightness and shortness of breath on days with visible smog in her city. She reported that her symptoms flared up, particularly on high-pollution days, even with minor exertion. Without recent changes in her physical activity or medication, her cardiologist suspected that exposure to particulate matter (PM2.5) was causing oxidative stress and inflammation, contributing to further endothelial dysfunction and narrowing of her coronary arteries. She was encouraged to limit outdoor exposure on polluted days, use an air-filtering mask when necessary, and monitor her symptoms closely with regular follow-up visits.
