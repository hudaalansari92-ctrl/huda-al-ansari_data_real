Kevin, a 48-year-old executive in a high-pressure business environment, had a history of mild hypertension, chronic stress, and occasional smoking, though there was no significant family history of heart disease. His fast-paced lifestyle involved long work hours, high stress, poor dietary habits centered around fast food and energy drinks, and no regular exercise. Kevin initially experienced chest tightness during stressful periods at work, particularly after long meetings or deadlines, but dismissed it as anxiety. Over the following months, the chest pain became more frequent, and he noticed shortness of breath and lightheadedness during physical activities, such as walking up stairs or carrying heavy objects. During a business trip, a severe episode of chest pain led to an emergency hospital visit. An ECG showed non-specific changes and elevated cardiac enzymes confirmed heart strain. A coronary angiogram revealed a 70% blockage in his right coronary artery and a 50% blockage in his left coronary artery. Kevin underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with a stent placement in his right coronary artery and was started on medications, including beta-blockers, statins, and aspirin. His cardiologist strongly advised him to adopt lifestyle changes, emphasizing stress management techniques like counseling and mindfulness exercises. Kevin also began a regular exercise routine, improved his diet, and reduced his work hours to manage his stress. These changes, combined with his medication regimen, helped him avoid further heart complications and improved his overall health.
