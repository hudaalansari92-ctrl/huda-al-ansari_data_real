A 77-year-old male with a history of diabetes, hypertension, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) was admitted for evaluation of worsening dyspnea.
Initial Evaluation: The patient reported chest discomfort and fatigue. An ECG showed atrial fibrillation with a rapid ventricular response.
Blood Tests: His lab results included:
Troponin: 0.1 ng/mL (elevated)
BNP (B-type natriuretic peptide): 400 pg/mL (indicating heart failure)
Creatinine: 1.5 mg/dL (suggesting renal impairment)
Echocardiogram: The echocardiogram indicated a reduced ejection fraction (EF) of 35% and significant left ventricular hypertrophy.
Coronary Angiography: This revealed severe three-vessel disease.
Management: The patient was counseled on the need for coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG) due to the complexity of his CAD and his comorbid conditions. He was also started on heart failure management medications.
