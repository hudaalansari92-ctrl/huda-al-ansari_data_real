A 45-year-old male named James, a senior manager in a fast-paced marketing firm, presented to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain and shortness of breath. He reported that the symptoms had worsened over the past few weeks, particularly during stressful work periods.
James described his chest pain as a tightness in the center of his chest that radiated to his left arm. He also experienced episodes of dizziness and fatigue, especially after long meetings or tight deadlines.
His risk factors included a high-stress job with long hours (often exceeding 60 hours per week), a sedentary lifestyle, and poor dietary habits (frequently eating fast food). He had a family history of heart disease; his father had a heart attack at age 50.
After diagnostic testing confirmed significant coronary artery disease, James underwent angioplasty and was placed on a regimen of medications to manage his cholesterol and blood pressure. He was also advised to implement stress management techniques and improve his diet.
