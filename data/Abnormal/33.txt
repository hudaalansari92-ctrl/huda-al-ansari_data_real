James, a 50-year-old construction worker, had struggled with obesity for most of his adult life. He also had high blood pressure and high cholesterol but had not been consistent with taking his medications. His physically demanding job often left him feeling fatigued, but he chalked it up to his weight and work. One morning, after lifting heavy materials at work, James felt an intense pressure in his chest, accompanied by nausea and sweating. His coworkers called emergency services, and he was taken to the hospital, where tests confirmed he had suffered a heart attack. A coronary angiogram revealed a 95% blockage in his right coronary artery. James underwent emergency percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI), where a stent was placed to open the blocked artery. After the procedure, James was placed on multiple medications, including statins, aspirin, and beta-blockers. His doctors strongly advised him to adopt a healthier lifestyle, emphasizing the importance of weight loss, regular exercise, and a heart-healthy diet. With support from a nutritionist and a weight-loss program, James began to lose weight and control his risk factors, significantly reducing his chances of further heart disease.
