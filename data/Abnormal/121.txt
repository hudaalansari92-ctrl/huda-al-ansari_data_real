A 65-year-old male diagnosed with obstructive sleep apnea presented to the cardiology clinic after experiencing recurrent episodes of chest pain and fatigue. He had been non-compliant with his CPAP therapy due to discomfort.
His risk factors included obesity (BMI of 32), a family history of heart disease, and hypertension, which was poorly controlled.
A cardiac evaluation, including an ECG, showed left ventricular hypertrophy. A subsequent stress test indicated inducible ischemia, leading to coronary angiography that confirmed significant coronary artery disease.
The patient was started on medications for hypertension and underwent PCI. He was also referred back to sleep medicine for better management of his sleep apnea, emphasizing the importance of compliance with CPAP therapy.
