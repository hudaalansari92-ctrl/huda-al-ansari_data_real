Emily is a 47-year-old marketing executive who lives in a city with high levels of pollution due to traffic and industrial emissions. She has a history of mild asthma and seasonal allergies, which have been exacerbated by poor air quality. Recently, Emily began experiencing episodes of chest tightness and shortness of breath, particularly on days when air pollution levels were elevated. After consulting her physician, she underwent a cardiac evaluation, which revealed increased arterial stiffness and signs of myocardial ischemia. The doctor explained that acute arterial vasoconstriction triggered by air pollution exposure could be a contributing factor to her symptoms. With a family history of coronary artery disease, Emily was advised to minimize her outdoor activities during high-pollution days, adopt a heart-healthy diet, and start a medication regimen to help manage her cardiovascular risk.
