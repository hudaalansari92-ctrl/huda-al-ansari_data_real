Omar, a 55-year-old retail store manager, had multiple chronic conditions that increased his risk of coronary heart disease (CHD), including type 2 diabetes for 10 years, hypertension for 8 years, and high cholesterol for 5 years. Despite being on medication, his blood sugar and blood pressure were poorly controlled due to inconsistent adherence to treatment and an unhealthy lifestyle. His sedentary habits, diet high in processed foods, and obesity (BMI 31), combined with high work-related stress, further elevated his risk. Over the past six months, he experienced frequent chest discomfort, especially after minimal exertion, along with dizziness, fatigue, and shortness of breath. Concerned about his worsening symptoms, he sought medical attention. Diagnostic tests revealed elevated LDL cholesterol, low HDL cholesterol, high fasting blood sugar, myocardial ischemia on ECG, and significant arterial blockages: 75% in the left anterior descending (LAD) artery and 60% in the right coronary artery (RCA). He was diagnosed with coronary artery disease (CAD) due to long-term uncontrolled diabetes, hypertension, and high cholesterol. His treatment plan included medication adjustments to better control his conditions, lifestyle modifications such as a low-fat, low-sodium, and low-sugar diet, increased physical activity, and stress management. Given the severity of his blockages, he underwent coronary angioplasty with stent placement to restore blood flow. After six months, Omar’s symptoms improved significantly, his blood pressure and blood sugar stabilized, he lost 5 kg, and he became more active while adhering to his prescribed medications.
