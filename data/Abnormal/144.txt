A 62-year-old female named Maria lived in a densely populated urban area with high levels of air pollution. She presented to her primary care physician with complaints of chest pain and shortness of breath, especially during outdoor activities.
Maria described her chest pain as tightness that worsened when she walked her dog in the neighborhood. She also experienced fatigue and frequent coughing, particularly on days when air quality was poor.
Chronic Diseases: Maria had a history of asthma and hypertension, both of which were exacerbated by her living environment. She had been using her inhaler more frequently due to increased respiratory symptoms.
Her risk factors included long-term exposure to air pollutants such as particulate matter (PM2.5) and nitrogen dioxide (NO2) from traffic emissions. Additionally, she had a family history of heart disease; her father had suffered a heart attack at age 65.
After diagnostic testing confirmed significant coronary artery disease, Maria was started on medications to manage her blood pressure and cholesterol levels. She was advised to limit outdoor activities on days with poor air quality and encouraged to consider relocating to a less polluted area for better health outcomes.
