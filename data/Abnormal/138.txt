A 30-year-old male named Alex came to the clinic for evaluation after experiencing episodes of chest discomfort during exercise. He was concerned because his father had suffered a heart attack at age 45.
Symptoms: Alex described his chest discomfort as a sharp pain that occurred primarily when he engaged in vigorous activities such as running or playing basketball. He also reported increased fatigue during workouts.
His risk factors included a family history of early heart disease, sedentary behavior during work hours as an IT professional, and poor dietary choices (high in processed foods). He was also a former smoker who quit five years ago.
After diagnostic testing showed no significant coronary artery disease but some signs of early atherosclerosis, Alex was advised on lifestyle modifications, including diet changes and regular exercise. He was closely monitored for any changes in symptoms or risk factors.
