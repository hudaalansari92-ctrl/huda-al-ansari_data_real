Linda, a 61-year-old woman who worked in retail, spent long hours standing on her feet at a busy department store. Her job often required her to work overtime, and she had little time for breaks or healthy meals during her shifts. Over the past few months, she started experiencing chest pain and discomfort while walking through the store or lifting heavy merchandise. The pain, which felt like pressure in her chest, sometimes radiated to her arms and neck. Given the fast-paced nature of her job, Linda dismissed the symptoms as fatigue and overexertion. After experiencing a more severe episode of chest pain while helping a customer, she decided to visit her doctor. Due to her age, work environment, and symptoms, coronary artery disease (CAD) was suspected. A subsequent evaluation revealed significant narrowing in her coronary arteries. Linda underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement. She was advised to make dietary changes, reduce stress, and engage in light exercise outside of her demanding job to improve her heart health.
