A 48-year-old female nurse experienced a myocardial infarction and underwent successful percutaneous coronary intervention with stent placement.
Occupational Stress: The patient reported high job demands, including long shifts, frequent overtime, and emotionally taxing interactions with patients and families. She felt she had little control over her work schedule and tasks.
Medical History: In addition to the recent heart attack, the patient had a history of hypertension and obesity.
Recurrent Events: Despite initial treatment, the patient suffered from recurrent angina and was found to have in-stent restenosis on follow-up angiography. She experienced another myocardial infarction 2 years later.
Management: The patient's medications were optimized, and she underwent repeat revascularization. She was referred to a cardiac rehabilitation program and a therapist to help manage her occupational stress.
Outcome: The patient made some adjustments to her work schedule but continued to experience significant job strain. She remained at high risk for future cardiovascular events.
