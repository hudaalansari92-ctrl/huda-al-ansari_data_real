David, a 60-year-old accountant, had a long history of high cholesterol and a family history of heart disease; his father had passed away from a heart attack at 62. Despite being aware of his risks, David was inconsistent in managing his health. He often skipped his cholesterol-lowering medications and led a largely sedentary lifestyle, compounded by stress from his job. Over the years, he gained weight and experienced occasional shortness of breath, but he attributed it to getting older. One day, while walking his dog, David experienced a sharp, crushing pain in his chest that radiated to his left arm and jaw. Feeling lightheaded and nauseous, he immediately sought medical attention. At the hospital, an ECG revealed that David was having a heart attack. A coronary angiogram later showed severe blockages in his coronary arteries, with a 95% blockage in his left anterior descending (LAD) artery and additional blockages in his right coronary artery. Due to the extent of his disease, David underwent emergency coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG). Post-surgery, he was placed on strict medication management, including statins, beta-blockers, and aspirin, and was advised to follow a heart-healthy diet and exercise regularly. His recovery was successful, and David committed to significant lifestyle changes to reduce his risk of further cardiac events.
