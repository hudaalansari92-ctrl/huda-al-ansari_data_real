A 13-year-old boy presented to the cardiology emergency department with complaints of shortness of breath, sweating, and chest pain radiating to both shoulders and neck. His father reported that the patient had been experiencing shortness of breath on minimal exertion for the past two weeks.
The patient exhibited severe chest pain, shortness of breath, and sweating during episodes of exertion. He also had multiple xanthomas (fatty deposits) on his skin.
The boy had a significant family history of hypercholesterolemia; his father had high cholesterol levels (350 mg/dL), and his grandfather suffered a sudden cardiac death due to premature CAD. His weight was 45 kg with a height of 123 cm, giving him a BMI of 29.7 kg/m².
He was diagnosed with premature CAD, leading to non-ST-elevation myocardial infarction secondary to familial hypercholesterolemia. A CT angiogram showed triple vessel disease, and he underwent percutaneous coronary angioplasty.
