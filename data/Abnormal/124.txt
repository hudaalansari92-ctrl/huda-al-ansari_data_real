A 70-year-old male with a long history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) presented to the emergency department with worsening shortness of breath and chest discomfort that intensified during exertion. He had a significant smoking history, having smoked one pack a day for over 30 years before quitting five years ago.
The patient reported persistent cough, increased sputum production, and episodes of wheezing. He also experienced chest tightness that worsened with physical activity and was often accompanied by palpitations.
His risk factors included a family history of heart disease, advanced age, and a sedentary lifestyle. He was also overweight, with a BMI of 29.
After undergoing diagnostic imaging and cardiac evaluation, he was found to have significant stenosis in the left anterior descending artery. The patient underwent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) to alleviate the blockage. He was started on bronchodilators for his COPD and was advised on lifestyle changes to improve both his respiratory and cardiovascular health.
