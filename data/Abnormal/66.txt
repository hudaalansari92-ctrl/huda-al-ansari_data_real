A 62-year-old male diagnosed with obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) presented to the cardiology clinic after experiencing recurrent episodes of chest pain and fatigue. He was compliant with his continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) therapy but expressed concerns about his cardiovascular health. A cardiac evaluation, including an ECG and echocardiogram, revealed left ventricular hypertrophy and signs consistent with ischemic heart disease. A subsequent stress test confirmed inducible ischemia. The patient was diagnosed with coronary artery disease and began treatment that included lifestyle changes, optimization of CPAP therapy, and medication for heart failure management. He was closely monitored for cardiovascular events.
