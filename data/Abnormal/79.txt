Mark, a 58-year-old male with a 30-year history of smoking, presented to his doctor with complaints of increasing chest pain and shortness of breath over the past few months. The pain, which occurred during physical exertion like climbing stairs or walking for more than 10 minutes, was described as a pressure-like sensation in the center of his chest, often radiating to his left arm. Mark had ignored the symptoms initially, but as the episodes became more frequent and occurred even at rest, he sought medical attention. His long smoking history, coupled with a sedentary lifestyle and poor diet, placed him at high risk for coronary artery disease (CAD). After being evaluated by a cardiologist, it was determined that Mark had significant blockages in his coronary arteries, which had been progressively limiting blood flow to his heart. He was advised to undergo percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with stent placement to restore blood flow and prevent future heart attacks. Post-procedure, Mark's symptoms improved, and he committed to quitting smoking and adopting a healthier lifestyle to reduce his risk of further heart problems.
