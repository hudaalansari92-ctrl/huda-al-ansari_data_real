A 58-year-old male factory worker presented with chest pain and was diagnosed with unstable angina.
Occupational Stress: The patient reported high physical demands at work, including heavy lifting and repetitive motions. He felt his efforts were not adequately rewarded, with limited opportunities for advancement and stagnant wages.
Risk Factors: The patient had a history of smoking, obesity, and type 2 diabetes. His diabetes was poorly controlled due to irregular work schedules and limited access to healthy food options.
Diagnostic Tests: Cardiac catheterization revealed multivessel coronary artery disease. An echocardiogram showed a preserved ejection fraction.
Management: The patient underwent coronary artery bypass grafting. He was started on medications to control his diabetes, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia. He was counseled on smoking cessation and a heart-healthy diet.
Outcome: The patient struggled to make lifestyle changes due to the demands of his job and limited social support. He experienced recurrent angina and another myocardial infarction within 5 years of his initial presentation.
