Mark, a 55-year-old man, has lived near a busy highway in a major city for over 20 years. His apartment is located close to the main road, where heavy traffic produces constant air pollution from vehicle exhaust. Over time, Mark begins to notice chronic respiratory issues, such as frequent coughing and shortness of breath, even though he doesn't smoke. The constant exposure to fine particulate matter and pollutants in the air leads to chronic inflammation in his lungs and blood vessels. This ongoing inflammation not only affects his respiratory health but also puts stress on his cardiovascular system. Despite his respiratory symptoms, Mark continues living in the same area, unaware of the long-term impact on his heart. Eventually, the inflammation in his arteries contributes to the buildup of plaque, a condition known as atherosclerosis. As the arteries narrow, his blood flow is restricted, and he begins experiencing occasional chest pain and fatigue. After a thorough medical examination, Mark is diagnosed with coronary artery disease (CAD), a condition linked to both his chronic inflammation from air pollution and the damage to his cardiovascular system over the years. His prolonged exposure to polluted air significantly increased his risk of developing heart disease.
