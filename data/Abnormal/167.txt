A 59-year-old woman with a history of coronary artery disease (CAD) was admitted to the hospital after experiencing severe chest pain and shortness of breath while visiting a heavily polluted industrial area. Despite being on medication for CAD, including antiplatelet therapy, her symptoms intensified after several days of exposure to high pollution levels. Her cardiologist suspected that pollutant exposure had triggered increased coagulation and thrombosis, elevating her risk for clot formation within her coronary arteries. An emergency angiogram revealed a newly formed clot causing a partial blockage, which was promptly treated. Following her recovery, she was advised to avoid high-pollution environments and to use protective measures, such as an N95 mask, when exposed to air pollutants.
