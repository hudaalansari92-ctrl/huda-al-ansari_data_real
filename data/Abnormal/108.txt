A 54-year-old woman with a history of Graves' disease, an autoimmune disorder that affects the thyroid and often causes chronic inflammation, presented to the emergency department with chest tightness and shortness of breath. Despite lacking traditional coronary artery disease (CAD) risk factors, she did not smoke, had normal cholesterol levels, maintained a healthy weight, and had no family history of heart disease, and her Graves' disease had been difficult to control over the years, leading to recurring inflammation and metabolic disturbances. Diagnostic tests, including coronary angiography, revealed a 70% blockage in one of her coronary arteries, confirming CAD that required stent placement. Her cardiologist explained that the chronic inflammation and immune activity associated with Graves' disease likely accelerated plaque buildup in her arteries. Post-treatment, she was advised to work with her endocrinologist to stabilize her thyroid function, adopt an anti-inflammatory diet, and undergo regular cardiovascular monitoring to reduce the risk of future cardiac events.
