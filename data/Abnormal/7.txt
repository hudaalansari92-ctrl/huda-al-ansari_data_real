An 88-year-old female was admitted for pain and swelling in her left arm. Her history included multiple interventions for CAD, including angioplasty and stent placements.
She had undergone successful stenting of the circumflex artery but faced complications with the right coronary artery, which remained occluded.
Despite initial success, follow-up angiography revealed significant occlusions and irregularities in her coronary arteries, necessitating further interventions.
The patient was placed on a regimen of statins and other lipid-lowering medications. She remained asymptomatic until 2011 when she presented with angina and was found to have significant in-stent restenosis and multiple coronary obstructions.
The patient's case illustrates the challenges of managing CAD in elderly patients, particularly with multiple comorbidities and the risk of restenosis.
