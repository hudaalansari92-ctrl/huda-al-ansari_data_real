Richard, a 59-year-old taxi driver, had been working long shifts for years, often spending 12-14 hours a day behind the wheel in heavy traffic. His job required him to sit for extended periods, and he rarely had the opportunity for regular physical activity. Additionally, his diet consisted mostly of fast food and takeout, which he ate between rides. Over time, Richard began noticing chest pain and shortness of breath, particularly when walking up stairs or carrying heavy bags. The pain, which felt like a squeezing sensation in his chest, became more frequent, but he ignored it, believing it was just a result of long hours at work. After experiencing chest pain while driving, he went to the emergency department. His physician suspected coronary artery disease (CAD) due to his sedentary job and poor diet. After further evaluation, Richard was diagnosed with significant blockages in his coronary arteries and underwent coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG). Following the surgery, he was advised to make lifestyle changes, including a healthier diet, taking breaks from driving to stretch, and incorporating regular exercise to prevent future cardiac events.
