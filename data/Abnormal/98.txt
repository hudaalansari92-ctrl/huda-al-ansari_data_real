A 55-year-old man with a history of rheumatoid arthritis (RA), an autoimmune condition known for causing joint inflammation and pain, visited his doctor after experiencing increasing chest discomfort and fatigue over the past month. Although he did not have traditional coronary artery disease (CAD) risk factors, his cholesterol levels, blood pressure, and weight were within healthy ranges, and he was a non-smoker, and his RA had been active for many years, leading to chronic inflammation despite treatment. Concerned about his symptoms, his physician ordered coronary angiography, which confirmed significant CAD with a 75% blockage in one of his major coronary arteries, requiring stent insertion. His cardiologist explained that chronic inflammation from RA likely accelerated the buildup of plaque in his arteries, increasing his heart disease risk independently of other factors. Following the procedure, he was advised to manage his RA closely, adhere to a heart-healthy lifestyle, and undergo regular cardiovascular monitoring to help prevent future complications.
