A 65-year-old retired truck driver with a long history of CAD reported worsening angina symptoms and intermittent shortness of breath after years of driving through high-pollution zones. Although he had managed his CAD with regular check-ups and medication, he suffered a mild heart attack after working in a highly polluted industrial area for several weeks. His cardiologist attributed the event to cumulative pollution exposure, which likely accelerated atherosclerotic plaque formation and increased plaque vulnerability. Following treatment, he was advised to limit his exposure to pollution, use a portable air purifier when necessary, and adhere closely to a heart-healthy lifestyle to help mitigate further risks.
