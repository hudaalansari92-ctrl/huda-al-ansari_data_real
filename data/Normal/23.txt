Saleem, A 35-year-old male visited his primary care physician complaining of intermittent chest pain and shortness of breath, particularly during stressful situations at work. He had a family history of anxiety disorders but no significant cardiovascular risk factors. An ECG and cardiac enzyme tests were performed, both of which returned normal results. A psychological evaluation indicated that his symptoms were consistent with panic attacks rather than cardiac issues. The patient was referred for cognitive-behavioral therapy and prescribed an anxiolytic medication. After addressing his anxiety, he reported a marked reduction in chest pain episodes.
