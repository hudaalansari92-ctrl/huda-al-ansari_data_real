Ahmed, a 47-year-old man, had high cholesterol and a sedentary lifestyle, but was a non-smoker and did not have diabetes or high blood pressure. He started experiencing crushing chest pain and shortness of breath when walking long distances or climbing stairs, which prompted him to see a doctor for fear of coronary artery disease. He underwent the necessary tests, including an electrocardiogram (ECG), a stress test, cardiac enzyme tests, and a coronary angiogram, but all results were normal and showed no blockages in the arteries. After further evaluation, he was diagnosed with endothelial dysfunction, a disorder that affects the ability of blood vessels to expand normally, leading to symptoms similar to angina without any actual blockages in the arteries. He was prescribed medications to improve blood vessel function, such as angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors and statins, in addition to recommendations to exercise regularly and improve diet. After several months of treatment and lifestyle changes, Ahmed noticed a significant improvement in his symptoms, and was able to perform his daily activities without feeling pain.
