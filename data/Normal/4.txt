Anna, a 38-year-old woman, presented to her primary care doctor with intermittent chest pain, palpitations, and episodes of lightheadedness. The chest pain was described as a dull ache, lasting for hours, and it did not seem related to physical exertion. Concerned about heart disease, her doctor conducted a full cardiac workup, including an ECG and blood tests, all of which were normal. However, an echocardiogram revealed a mild mitral valve prolapse (MVP), a condition where one of the heart's valves doesn't close properly, causing occasional palpitations and chest discomfort. Although her symptoms mimicked those of coronary artery disease, they were caused by the MVP, a generally benign condition. She was reassured that her heart was otherwise healthy and no further treatment was required aside from regular monitoring.
