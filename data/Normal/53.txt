Sara, a 50-year-old woman with no conventional risk factors for coronary heart disease (CHD), experienced intermittent chest pain, palpitations, and fatigue, particularly during periods of stress. Despite maintaining a healthy lifestyle with normal blood pressure and cholesterol levels, her symptoms led to further investigation. Diagnostic tests revealed elevated high-sensitivity C-reactive protein (hsCRP) levels, indicating chronic inflammation and signs of endothelial dysfunction and oxidative stress. Although she lacked traditional CHD risk factors, her physician diagnosed her with an elevated cardiovascular risk due to these nontraditional factors. Treatment included anti-inflammatory therapy, stress management techniques, an antioxidant-rich diet, and regular monitoring. After six months, Ms. Sara's symptoms improved, her hsCRP levels decreased, and she gained better control over her stress, underscoring the importance of addressing nontraditional risk factors in the prevention of CHD.
