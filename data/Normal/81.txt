A 30-year-old female named Emily sought medical advice for persistent fatigue and mild chest discomfort during stressful periods at work. She worked as an attorney in a high-pressure environment.
Emily described her chest discomfort as a dull ache that occurred primarily during intense workdays but resolved quickly with rest. She also reported feeling overwhelmed by work-related stress.
Her risk factors included high levels of occupational stress, a sedentary lifestyle, and irregular eating habits due to long hours at the office. She did not smoke or consume alcohol excessively.
After undergoing stress testing and cardiac evaluations that showed no evidence of CAD, Emily was reassured about her heart health. She was advised on stress management techniques and encouraged to incorporate physical activity into her routine.
