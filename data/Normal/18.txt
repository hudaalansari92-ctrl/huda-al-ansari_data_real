Salma, A 45-year-old female presented to the emergency department with complaints of severe chest pain that radiated to her left arm and jaw. Given her symptoms, there was initial concern for a myocardial infarction. The patient had a history of GERD and frequently experienced heartburn. She reported that the chest pain often occurred after meals and was associated with acid regurgitation. An electrocardiogram (ECG) showed no signs of ischemia, and cardiac enzyme tests were negative for myocardial infarction. A subsequent upper endoscopy confirmed esophagitis due to acid reflux. The patient was treated with proton pump inhibitors and lifestyle modifications, including dietary changes and weight loss. After treatment, her chest pain significantly decreased, confirming that her symptoms were due to GERD rather than CAD.
