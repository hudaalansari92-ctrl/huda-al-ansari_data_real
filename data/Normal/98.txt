A 40-year-old female named Lisa worked in an office located near a busy road. She presented with complaints of fatigue, headaches, and mild chest discomfort after long hours at work.
Lisa described her chest discomfort as a dull ache that occurred mainly at the end of the day after prolonged exposure to indoor air pollution from traffic outside. She also reported frequent headaches that worsened in the afternoons.
She had no significant chronic diseases but experienced occasional anxiety related to work stress.
Her risk factors included living in an urban area with high traffic congestion and spending most of her day indoors near windows facing the road. She also had a family history of asthma but no personal history of heart disease.
After evaluations confirmed no coronary artery disease, Lisa was advised on workplace modifications such as improving ventilation and using air purifiers. She was educated about the effects of indoor air quality on health and encouraged to take breaks outdoors when pollution levels were lower.
