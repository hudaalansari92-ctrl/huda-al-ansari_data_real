Rose, A 50-year-old female with obesity (BMI of 30) came in for an annual health assessment. Despite her weight, she had normal lipid levels, blood pressure, and glucose levels. She led an active lifestyle and had no family history of heart disease. She reported occasional fatigue but attributed it to her busy lifestyle rather than any cardiac concern. Comprehensive metabolic panels and an echocardiogram were conducted due to her obesity status. All results returned within normal ranges, indicating no signs of cardiovascular disease. The patient was categorized as "metabolically healthy obese" and received counseling on maintaining her health through diet and exercise without any indication of developing CAD.
