Saad, a 45-year-old man, suffers from chest pain described as pressure or heaviness, especially when exerting himself physically, such as climbing stairs or walking long distances. The pain sometimes radiates to the left arm and lower jaw, accompanied by feelings of fatigue and shortness of breath. The symptoms improve with rest, but he also suffers from recurrent episodes of dizziness and cold sweats. Although he does not have a history of heart disease, high blood pressure, or diabetes, does not smoke, and is not obese, he has a family history of heart disease, as his father suffered a heart attack at the age of fifty. The initial tests, including an electrocardiogram, stress test, blood tests, and coronary angiography, showed normal results with no signs of myocardial ischemia or coronary artery blockage. Based on these results, coronary artery disease was ruled out, and Saad was diagnosed with non-cardiac chest pain syndrome, which may be caused by factors such as muscle spasms, gastroesophageal reflux, anxiety disorders, or other reasons like gastrointestinal issues or pneumonia. The treatment included additional tests to determine the exact cause, such as endoscopy or psychological evaluation, with the prescription of antacids if the cause was gastroesophageal reflux, relaxation exercises and cognitive-behavioral therapy if anxiety was the main factor.
