A 55-year-old male named John worked in construction and lived near a busy highway. He visited the clinic complaining of a chronic cough and increased fatigue over the past few months.
John reported that his cough was persistent and worsened during the winter months when air quality was particularly poor. He also experienced occasional chest tightness but denied any significant chest pain or shortness of breath at rest.
He had no significant chronic diseases but had experienced seasonal allergies that exacerbated his respiratory symptoms.
His risk factors included long-term exposure to dust and particulate matter from construction sites, as well as vehicle emissions from the nearby highway. He also had a family history of respiratory issues but no history of heart disease.
Diagnostic tests ruled out coronary artery disease, but John's chronic cough was attributed to environmental exposure and possible allergic reactions. He was advised on protective measures at work, including wearing masks during construction activities and reducing exposure to outdoor pollutants.
