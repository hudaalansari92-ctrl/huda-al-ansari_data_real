Salah, A 60-year-old male with obstructive sleep apnea was referred for evaluation due to excessive daytime sleepiness and fatigue. He had been using continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) therapy for his condition but expressed concerns about his heart health due to his sleep disorder. A cardiac evaluation, including an ECG and echocardiogram, was performed as part of his assessment. Both tests returned normal results, showing no signs of coronary artery disease.  The patient continued his CPAP therapy and was educated on the importance of managing sleep apnea to reduce potential cardiovascular risks. He remained free from coronary artery disease throughout follow-up visits.
