A 30-year-old male named Kevin worked as an outdoor sports instructor in a city known for its high traffic congestion. He visited his doctor due to frequent headaches, fatigue, and occasional chest discomfort after outdoor activities.
Kevin reported experiencing tightness in his chest after strenuous activities outdoors, especially on days when pollution levels were high. He also complained of headaches that he attributed to poor air quality.
 He had no chronic diseases but suffered from seasonal allergies that were exacerbated by environmental factors.
His risk factors included living in an area with heavy traffic emissions and being exposed to high pollen levels during certain seasons. He led an active lifestyle but often found himself outdoors during peak pollution times due to his job.
After a thorough evaluation showed no signs of coronary artery disease, Kevin was advised on strategies to manage his allergies and reduce exposure to pollutants during outdoor activities. He was encouraged to check local air quality reports before planning outdoor exercises.
