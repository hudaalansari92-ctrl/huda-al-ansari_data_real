Angela, A 72-year-old female with peripheral artery disease (PAD) presented with leg pain during exertion but denied any chest pain or other cardiac symptoms. She had a history of smoking and was being treated for intermittent claudication. Due to her PAD diagnosis and risk factors, she underwent a cardiac evaluation that included an ECG and echocardiogram. Both tests showed normal results, indicating no evidence of coronary artery disease. The patient was treated for her PAD with lifestyle modifications and supervised exercise therapy. She was monitored regularly for cardiovascular health and did not develop CAD.
