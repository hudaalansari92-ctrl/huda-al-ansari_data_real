Leila, a 60-year-old woman, suffers from several chronic diseases that increase her likelihood of developing coronary artery disease, such as type 2 diabetes, which she has been suffering from for 15 years, and chronic hypertension. Additionally, she has a long history of high cholesterol levels and has not adhered to the medication regimen regularly. Despite that, Leila did not suffer from any symptoms indicating the presence of coronary artery disease, such as angina or shortness of breath, and she has not been diagnosed with the disease so far. Based on her health condition and multiple risk factors, her doctor recommended adopting a comprehensive plan to improve her lifestyle, which includes regulating blood sugar and blood pressure levels, improving her diet, and increasing daily physical activity, in addition to regular medical follow-ups to prevent the development of coronary artery disease in the future.
