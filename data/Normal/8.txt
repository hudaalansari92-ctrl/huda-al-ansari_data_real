Melissa, a 49-year-old woman with obesity and a history of high cholesterol, presented with chest pain and a burning sensation in her throat, especially after meals. The pain sometimes radiated to her back, which made her worry about heart disease. Blood tests and an ECG revealed no abnormalities, and her echocardiogram showed normal heart function. After ruling out coronary artery disease, Melissa was diagnosed with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). The burning sensation was caused by stomach acid refluxing into her esophagus, mimicking heart-related symptoms. She was prescribed proton pump inhibitors and advised to lose weight, which significantly alleviated her symptoms.
