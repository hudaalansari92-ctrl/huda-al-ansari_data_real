A 58-year-old female patient with a notable history of coronary heart disease (CHD) in her family presented for a routine check-up. Her father had experienced multiple myocardial infarctions by age 60, and her brother was diagnosed with CAD at 55. The patient herself had several risk factors: she was hypertensive, had elevated cholesterol levels, and was classified as obese with a BMI of 32. Additionally, she had a sedentary lifestyle and reported occasional smoking in her younger years, though she quit over a decade ago. Despite these significant risk factors and her family history, comprehensive diagnostic tests, including a coronary angiogram, revealed no evidence of obstructive coronary artery disease. The absence of CAD was surprising, given her profile, prompting further exploration into nontraditional risk factors that might contribute to her cardiovascular risk. This case illustrates the complexity of cardiovascular health, where patients can present multiple risk factors yet remain free of clinically significant CAD, highlighting the importance of individualized assessment and management strategies in cardiovascular care.
