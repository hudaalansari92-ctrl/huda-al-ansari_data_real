George, a 66-year-old male with a 25-year history of severe psoriasis, experienced occasional chest pain and palpitations. Psoriasis is a chronic inflammatory skin condition, and long-term inflammation can contribute to the development of atherosclerosis and increase the risk of coronary artery disease. Concerned about his symptoms, George underwent blood tests, an ECG, and a coronary angiogram. His blood tests, including inflammatory markers and cardiac enzymes, were normal, and his ECG showed no evidence of ischemia. The coronary angiogram revealed no significant blockages in his arteries. Despite his chronic inflammation from psoriasis, George was found to have no coronary artery disease. His chest pain was determined to be related to stress and anxiety, and he was reassured about his heart health. His dermatologist adjusted his psoriasis treatment to better manage the inflammation and reduce long-term cardiovascular risk.
