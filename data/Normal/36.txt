Maria, A 58-year-old female with a history of psoriatic arthritis presented for routine follow-up. She had been managing her condition with immunosuppressive therapy but expressed concerns about her cardiovascular health due to her chronic inflammatory disease. Given her history, she underwent a cardiac evaluation that included an ECG, echocardiogram, and stress testing. All results indicated normal cardiac function without any evidence of coronary artery disease. The patient continued her treatment for psoriatic arthritis while being monitored for cardiovascular health. She remained asymptomatic regarding CAD despite the potential risks associated with chronic inflammation.
