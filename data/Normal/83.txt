A 36-year-old male named Patient T worked as an investment banker in a high-pressure environment. He presented with complaints of chest tightness and anxiety during peak work periods.
Patient T reported episodes of chest tightness that occurred primarily during stressful meetings or long hours at work. He also experienced palpitations and excessive sweating during these episodes but denied any pain radiating to his arms or jaw.
His risk factors included high levels of occupational stress, a sedentary lifestyle due to long hours at his desk, poor dietary habits (frequent fast food consumption), and a family history of heart disease (his father had a heart attack at age 50).
After a thorough evaluation, it was determined that he did not have CAD but exhibited signs consistent with stress-induced angina. He was advised on stress management techniques and encouraged to incorporate physical activity into his routine.
