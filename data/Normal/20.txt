Henry, A 50-year-old male presented to his primary care physician with persistent chest pain that he described as a dull ache on the left side of his chest. He had recently started a new exercise regimen that included heavy lifting at the gym. The pain worsened with certain movements and palpation of the costosternal junction. An ECG was performed, which showed no abnormalities. Cardiac enzyme tests were negative for myocardial infarction. Physical examination revealed tenderness over the costochondral joints. The diagnosis of costochondritis was made, and the patient was treated with NSAIDs and advised to modify his exercise routine. After a few weeks, his symptoms resolved completely.
