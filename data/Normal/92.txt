A 55-year-old construction worker complained of feeling short of breath and experiencing mild chest pain after long shifts on heavily polluted sites. Though he had no family history of heart disease and his tests showed no current signs of CAD, his doctor suggested that his continuous exposure to dust and PM2.5 from construction work was leading to oxidative stress and inflammation, increasing his long-term risk for atherosclerosis. He was advised to wear a high-efficiency particulate mask, reduce work hours in high-exposure zones if possible, and adopt a diet high in leafy greens, berries, and other antioxidant-rich foods.
