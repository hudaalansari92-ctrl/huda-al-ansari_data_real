A 65-year-old male with a long-standing history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) presented with increased shortness of breath and a persistent cough. He had a significant smoking history, having smoked one pack a day for over 30 years before quitting five years ago.
The patient reported wheezing, chest tightness, and fatigue that worsened with physical activity. He denied any episodes of chest pain or discomfort typically associated with heart disease.
His risk factors included advanced age, a sedentary lifestyle, and a family history of lung disease. He was also slightly overweight, with a BMI of 28.
After undergoing pulmonary function tests and imaging studies, it was confirmed that he had severe COPD but no evidence of coronary artery disease. He was treated with bronchodilators and corticosteroids to manage his respiratory symptoms.
