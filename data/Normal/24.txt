Hani, A 45-year-old male presented with persistent left-sided chest pain that worsened with movement and palpation. He was concerned about having heart disease due to the nature of his symptoms. An ECG showed no ischemic changes and cardiac enzymes were within normal limits. Physical examination revealed tenderness over the costosternal junction, leading to a diagnosis of costochondritis. The patient was treated with non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and advised on physical therapy exercises. His symptoms improved significantly over the following weeks.
