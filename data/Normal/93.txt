A 47-year-old man, an office worker who lived in a densely populated area with frequent high smog days, reported feeling fatigued and experiencing mild but frequent chest discomfort. Although he had a normal weight, no history of smoking, and no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease, his physician suspected that chronic exposure to air pollution, particularly PM2.5, was contributing to oxidative stress and inflammation. Initial tests showed no CAD, but he was advised to invest in an indoor air purifier, avoid exercising outdoors on high-pollution days, and consider adding turmeric and green tea to his diet to help manage inflammation and support endothelial function.
