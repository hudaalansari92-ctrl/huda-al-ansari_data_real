A 43-year-old female, referred to as Patient S, visited the clinic due to concerns about her weight. At weighing 220 pounds, she was classified as obese with an apple-shaped fat distribution.
Patient S reported fatigue and occasional joint pain but denied any chest pain or cardiovascular symptoms. She expressed concern about her health due to her weight.
Her significant risk factors included long-standing obesity, a sedentary lifestyle with little exercise, and poor dietary habits that did not adhere to cardiac nutrition recommendations.
Despite her obesity and associated risk factors, diagnostic tests revealed no signs of coronary artery disease. She was counseled on weight management strategies and encouraged to adopt a more active lifestyle.
