Ahmed, a 52-year-old man, began experiencing crushing chest pain that radiated to his left arm and lower jaw, especially when walking long distances or climbing stairs. The pain went away with rest, leading him to believe he had coronary artery disease. He went to the hospital, where he underwent several tests, including an electrocardiogram (ECG), stress test, and cardiac enzyme tests, all of which were normal. A coronary angiogram showed no blockages in the arteries, ruling out coronary artery disease. After reviewing his medical history and conducting further tests, he was diagnosed with microvascular angina, a condition that affects the small blood vessels of the heart and causes symptoms similar to angina without blockages in the major arteries. A treatment plan was put in place that included medications to improve blood flow, such as beta blockers and nitrates, as well as lifestyle tips, such as regular exercise, stress reduction, and a healthy diet. After several weeks of treatment, Ahmed felt a marked improvement in his symptoms, confirming that the pain was not caused by traditional coronary artery disease, but rather by dysfunction of the small blood vessels in the heart.
