Mary, a 65-year-old woman with long-standing hypertension and high cholesterol, came to the clinic with mild chest discomfort and occasional shortness of breath. She had been on medication for hypertension for over 10 years, and her cholesterol was only moderately controlled with statins. Given her age and risk factors, her doctor ordered blood tests, an ECG, and a stress echocardiogram. Her blood work, including troponin, was normal, and the stress test showed no signs of ischemia. Her echocardiogram showed a normal ejection fraction of 60%, and coronary angiography confirmed that she did not have significant blockages in her arteries. Mary was reassured that despite her risk factors, she did not have coronary artery disease. Her symptoms were related to mild anxiety and deconditioning, and she was encouraged to continue managing her hypertension and cholesterol with medication and lifestyle changes to maintain her heart health.
