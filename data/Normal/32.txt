Sandy, A 68-year-old female with a long-standing history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) presented to her primary care physician with complaints of increased shortness of breath and occasional chest tightness. She had a significant smoking history but had quit five years prior. Given her respiratory symptoms and risk factors, she underwent a comprehensive cardiac evaluation, including an ECG and echocardiogram. Both tests indicated normal cardiac function and a subsequent coronary angiogram revealed no significant coronary artery disease. The patient was diagnosed with COPD exacerbations and was treated with bronchodilators and corticosteroids. She was educated about her condition and continued to manage her respiratory health without developing CAD.
