A 70-year-old female presented with leg pain when walking short distances, which improved with rest. She had been diagnosed with peripheral artery disease two years prior but had not undergone any cardiac evaluations until now.
The patient described intermittent claudication in her calves during physical activity. She also mentioned occasional leg numbness but denied any chest pain or discomfort.
Her risk factors included a history of smoking (quit 8 years ago), hyperlipidemia, and diabetes mellitus type 2. She also had a family history of cardiovascular diseases.
After vascular imaging confirmed significant narrowing in the peripheral arteries but no evidence of coronary artery disease, she was treated for her PAD with lifestyle modifications and supervised exercise therapy.
