A 44-year-old female patient with a diagnosis of ulcerative colitis presented for evaluation due to persistent fatigue and occasional palpitations but had no typical risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD) such as hypertension, diabetes, or high cholesterol. Her medical history revealed chronic inflammation associated with her autoimmune condition, which had been managed with immunosuppressive therapy. Despite her relatively healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and a balanced diet, she experienced significant stress related to her ongoing health challenges. Laboratory tests indicated elevated inflammatory markers, including C-reactive protein (CRP), suggesting systemic inflammation. A comprehensive cardiac assessment, including echocardiography and stress testing, showed normal coronary arteries with no evidence of CAD.
