A 48-year-old male patient with a diagnosis of ankylosing spondylitis (AS) presented for evaluation due to persistent fatigue and mild chest discomfort. He had no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), such as hypertension, diabetes, or hyperlipidemia, and maintained a healthy weight and active lifestyle. However, his medical history was significant for chronic inflammation associated with AS, which had been managed with nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and biologic therapy. Despite his efforts to control his condition, laboratory tests revealed elevated inflammatory markers, including C-reactive protein (CRP), indicating ongoing systemic inflammation. A thorough cardiac evaluation, including a stress test and coronary angiography, showed normal coronary arteries with no evidence of CAD.
