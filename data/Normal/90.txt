A 42-year-old non-smoking man who commuted daily by bike along a major highway began noticing increased fatigue and occasional chest discomfort after rides on days with visible pollution. Despite having no traditional risk factors for CAD, his doctor noted that daily exposure to high levels of PM2.5 from traffic could be contributing to oxidative stress and low-grade inflammation, potentially leading to early endothelial dysfunction. Although his initial cardiovascular tests were normal, he was advised to adjust his commute to lower-traffic times, wear an air-filtering mask while biking, and include anti-inflammatory supplements, such as omega-3 fatty acids, to support vascular health.
