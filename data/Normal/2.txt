Maria, a 45-year-old woman, presented to the clinic with recurrent episodes of chest tightness, palpitations, shortness of breath, and dizziness. These episodes often occurred during stressful situations or without warning, and she feared they were signs of a heart attack. However, her blood tests showed normal troponin levels, and her ECG and echocardiogram did not reveal any cardiac abnormalities. After a thorough evaluation and a referral to a mental health specialist, Maria was diagnosed with panic disorder. Her chest pain and other symptoms were anxiety-related rather than heart-related. She was started on anti-anxiety medications and cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), which helped manage her panic attacks, reducing her episodes of chest discomfort.
