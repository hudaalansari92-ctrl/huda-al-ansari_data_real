A 40-year-old woman with a history of chronic depression and generalised anxiety disorder visited her primary care physician due to frequent episodes of fatigue, poor sleep, and lack of motivation. Although she had no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), her blood pressure and cholesterol levels were normal, she was a non-smoker, and she maintained a healthy weight; her mental health conditions had led to sedentary habits and poor dietary choices, as she often relied on processed foods for convenience and comfort. Concerned about the long-term cardiovascular risks associated with depression and anxiety, her physician recommended a stress test and basic cardiovascular assessments, which revealed no evidence of CAD at this time. However, she was counselled on the importance of managing her mental health as part of her cardiovascular care and encouraged to pursue lifestyle changes, including regular physical activity and a balanced diet, to help mitigate the potential effects of psychological stress on her heart health.
