A 72-year-old female named Margaret lived in an urban area known for its high levels of air pollution. She presented to the emergency room with worsening asthma symptoms and fatigue.
Margaret described increased wheezing and shortness of breath that occurred more frequently when she went outside on days with high smog levels. She also reported feeling unusually tired and having difficulty sleeping due to her respiratory issues.
She had a long-standing history of asthma and mild hypertension but had never been diagnosed with coronary artery disease.
Her risk factors included living in a polluted environment, being overweight (BMI of 31), and having poorly controlled asthma exacerbated by air quality issues.
After treatment for her asthma exacerbation, including corticosteroids and bronchodilators, Margaret was educated about the importance of monitoring air quality levels. Although she did not have CAD, she was advised on lifestyle changes to improve her overall health and reduce her exposure to environmental pollutants.
