Farah, A 50-year-old female presented to the emergency department with severe chest pain that radiated to her left arm, mimicking classic angina. She had a history of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) but was concerned that her symptoms indicated a heart problem. An electrocardiogram (ECG) showed normal sinus rhythm, and cardiac enzyme tests were negative for myocardial infarction. A subsequent endoscopy revealed esophagitis, confirming that her chest pain was due to GERD rather than CAD. The patient was treated with proton pump inhibitors and lifestyle modifications, leading to significant relief from her symptoms.
