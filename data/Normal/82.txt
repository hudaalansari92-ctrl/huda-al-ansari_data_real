A 43-year-old woman named Patient S visited her doctor for evaluation after experiencing episodes of chest discomfort during physical activity. She was concerned about her heart health due to her weight and family history.
Patient S described her chest discomfort as a pressure-like sensation that occurred when climbing stairs or during stressful situations. She also reported fatigue and occasional shortness of breath.
Her risk factors included significant obesity (weighing 220 pounds), a sedentary lifestyle, and a family history of heart disease. She had little success in dieting over the years and did not exercise regularly.
Although she exhibited several risk factors for CAD, diagnostic tests revealed no significant coronary artery disease. She was counseled on lifestyle modifications, including dietary changes and regular exercise, to help reduce her risk.
