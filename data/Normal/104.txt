Huda is 60 years old and suffers from a pressing chest pain that occurs during physical activity such as walking or climbing stairs, disappears at rest, and is accompanied by a feeling of fatigue and shortness of breath. She also suffers from recurrent dizziness and palpitations. There is no previous history of heart disease, but the patient suffers from iron deficiency anemia and has a history of thyroid disorders (hypothyroidism) which is being treated with medication. The initial tests showed normal results for the ECG, stress test, and cardiac enzymes, but blood tests revealed a decrease in hemoglobin (9.5 g/dL). Additionally, the coronary angiography did not show any blockage in the coronary arteries. Based on that, the patient was diagnosed with anemia, where the lack of hemoglobin causes symptoms such as fatigue, shortness of breath, and chest pain due to the deficiency of oxygen reaching the tissues, including the heart muscle. Iron supplements were prescribed to treat anemia, along with dietary advice to increase the intake of iron-rich foods such as red meat, leafy vegetables, and legumes, in addition to follow-up with an endocrinologist to regulate thyroid hormone levels. Her condition will also be monitored regularly by a cardiologist to ensure that no other complications arise.
