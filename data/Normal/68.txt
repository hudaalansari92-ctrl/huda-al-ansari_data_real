A 52-year-old female presented for a routine check-up after being diagnosed with hyperlipidemia. Her lipid profile showed elevated total cholesterol levels, particularly high LDL cholesterol, but she had no history of chest pain or cardiovascular symptoms.
The patient reported feeling generally well but expressed concern about her cholesterol levels. She had occasional fatigue but attributed it to her busy lifestyle.
Her risk factors included a family history of heart disease (her mother had a heart attack at age 60), a sedentary lifestyle, and a high-stress job as an executive. She also reported irregular eating habits.
Despite her elevated cholesterol levels, cardiac evaluations, including stress testing, showed no evidence of coronary artery disease. She was advised on dietary changes and started on statin therapy to manage her hyperlipidemia.
