Ahmed, a 47-year-old overweight man (BMI 31) with a history of poorly controlled hypertension, recently developed crushing chest pain during exercise, with shortness of breath and unexplained fatigue. Given his risk factors, an electrocardiogram (ECG) was performed, which showed no changes indicative of ischemia, and the results of a stress test were normal. Blood tests showed elevated cholesterol levels, but cardiac enzymes were normal. A coronary angiogram was performed, which showed normal blood flow with no blockages in the arteries. Upon further evaluation, he was diagnosed with pulmonary hypertension, an increase in blood pressure in the pulmonary arteries that can cause symptoms similar to coronary artery disease. Treatment with vasodilators and diuretics, along with lifestyle modifications, was initiated, which gradually improved his symptoms.
