A 42-year-old male patient with a diagnosis of psoriasis presented for evaluation due to persistent fatigue and mild chest discomfort. He had a significant history of chronic inflammation associated with his autoimmune condition but had no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD) such as hypertension, diabetes, or hyperlipidemia. The patient was not a smoker and maintained a relatively active lifestyle, yet he reported high levels of stress related to managing his psoriasis flares. Laboratory tests revealed elevated levels of inflammatory markers, including C-reactive protein (CRP), indicating ongoing systemic inflammation. Despite these findings, a comprehensive cardiac evaluation, including a coronary angiogram, showed no evidence of CAD.
