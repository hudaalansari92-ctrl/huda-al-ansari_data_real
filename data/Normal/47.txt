Fatima, a 48-years-old, was suffering from mild obesity and high blood pressure without a family history of heart disease. Although she doesn't smoke and engages in light walking, she started feeling chest pains after physical exertion or stressful situations, which made her worry about the possibility of having coronary artery disease. Any arterial blockage was ruled out after a series of medical tests, such as an electrocardiogram, a stress test, and an echocardiogram. The doctor confirmed that she does not suffer from coronary artery disease but rather from non-cardiac angina resulting from stress and muscle spasms in the chest. The doctor recommended lifestyle changes by following a healthy diet, walking daily for 30 minutes, and using relaxation techniques to reduce stress. After a month of treatment and lifestyle changes, her condition improved significantly, with the pain disappearing and blood pressure levels stabilizing. In summary, the symptoms that Mrs. Fatima experienced were similar to coronary artery disease. Still, they were related to stress and high blood pressure, and she was effectively treated through preventive lifestyle changes.
