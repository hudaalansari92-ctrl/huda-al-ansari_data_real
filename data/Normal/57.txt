A 48-year-old man with a history of obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) visited his doctor complaining of daytime fatigue, poor concentration, and frequent morning headaches. Despite lacking traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), he was a non-smoker, had normal cholesterol levels, and maintained healthy blood pressure; his sleep disorder had begun to affect his overall health. His wife reported loud snoring and episodes of interrupted breathing during sleep. Concerned about the potential impact of sleep apnea on cardiovascular health, his physician ordered a sleep study, confirming severe OSA with frequent oxygen desaturation during the night. While his cardiovascular tests, including an ECG and coronary calcium scan, showed no evidence of CAD, his doctor explained that untreated sleep apnea could contribute to future heart risks due to recurrent drops in blood oxygen and increased blood pressure spikes during sleep. He was advised to start using a CPAP machine to manage his apnea, adopt a regular exercise routine, and follow up regularly to monitor his heart health over time.
