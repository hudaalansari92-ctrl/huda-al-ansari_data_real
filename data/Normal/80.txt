A 60-year-old female named Linda presented for a follow-up regarding her rheumatoid arthritis (RA). She expressed concerns about the potential cardiovascular risks associated with her chronic inflammatory condition.
Linda reported joint pain and occasional fatigue but denied any chest pain or palpitations. She was particularly worried about the long-term effects of RA on her heart health.
Her risk factors included chronic inflammation from RA, obesity (BMI of 29), and a family history of heart disease. She also had elevated stress levels due to managing her condition.
After comprehensive evaluations that included imaging studies showing no signs of CAD, it was confirmed that she did not have coronary artery disease. Linda was educated on the importance of managing her RA effectively while being monitored for cardiovascular health.
