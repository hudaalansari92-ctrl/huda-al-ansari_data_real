A 42-year-old woman with a 10-year history of psoriasis visited her doctor complaining of fatigue and occasional chest discomfort. Despite lacking traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), she was a non-smoker, had a normal BMI, normal cholesterol levels, and no family history of heart disease; her physician noted the potential cardiovascular risks associated with her autoimmune condition. Psoriasis, characterized by chronic systemic inflammation, is known to increase the risk of vascular inflammation, which can impact heart health over time. Concerned about the potential effects of ongoing inflammation, her doctor ordered a stress test and other cardiovascular assessments, which showed no evidence of CAD. However, she was advised to closely manage her psoriasis with anti-inflammatory treatments, adopt a heart-healthy lifestyle, and schedule regular follow-ups to monitor her heart health, given the connection between chronic inflammation from autoimmune conditions and increased cardiovascular risk.
