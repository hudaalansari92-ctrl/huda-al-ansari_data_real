Saleem, A 60-year-old male with a long history of type 2 diabetes mellitus presented for evaluation after experiencing episodes of chest discomfort. He had been managing his diabetes for over 15 years and was compliant with his medication regimen, which included metformin and statin. Despite his chronic condition, he had normal blood pressure and a well-controlled HbA1c level of 6.5%. During a routine coronary angiogram to investigate his chest symptoms, it was found that he had no significant coronary artery disease. The angiogram revealed clear coronary arteries with no stenosis greater than 20%. The patient was reassured that his symptoms were likely related to non-cardiac causes, such as anxiety or gastrointestinal issues. He continued to manage his diabetes effectively and maintained regular follow-ups without developing CAD.
