A 48-year-old female named Sarah visited the clinic due to increased anxiety and occasional chest discomfort. She was worried about her heart health because her mother had suffered from CAD.
Sarah described her chest discomfort as a tightness that would occur during stressful moments but would resolve with rest. She also reported feelings of anxiety and difficulty sleeping.
Her risk factors included a family history of heart disease, obesity (BMI of 30), and a sedentary lifestyle. She worked long hours in an office setting and often skipped meals due to her busy schedule.
After extensive evaluation, including cardiac imaging that showed no blockages, it was confirmed that she did not have CAD. Sarah was referred for counseling to manage her anxiety and was encouraged to adopt healthier lifestyle habits.
