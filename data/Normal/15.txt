John, a 55-year-old male with stage 3 chronic kidney disease (CKD) and a 20-year history of hypertension, experienced chest pain and fatigue, particularly during physical activity. Given that CKD is a known risk factor for coronary artery disease, his doctor conducted a series of tests to assess his heart health. Blood tests, including troponin and kidney function tests, were within normal ranges. His ECG and echocardiogram showed no signs of ischemia, and his ejection fraction was normal at 55%. A coronary CT angiogram showed no significant coronary artery blockages. Despite the high risk associated with CKD and hypertension, John was found to have no coronary artery disease. His chest pain was attributed to mild anemia related to CKD and was managed with iron supplements and dietary changes.
