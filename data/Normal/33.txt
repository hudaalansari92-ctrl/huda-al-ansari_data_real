James, A 54-year-old male presented for a routine check-up after being diagnosed with hyperlipidemia. His lipid profile showed elevated LDL cholesterol levels, and he had a family history of heart disease. Despite these risk factors, he reported no symptoms such as chest pain or discomfort. The physician recommended a stress test and coronary angiography to evaluate his heart health. The angiogram revealed clear coronary arteries with no signs of atherosclerosis. The patient was started on statin therapy to manage his cholesterol levels and was advised to adopt lifestyle changes, including dietary modifications and increased physical activity. He remained free from coronary artery disease during follow-up evaluations.
