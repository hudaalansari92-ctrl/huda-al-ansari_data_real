A 35-year-old male named John presented to his primary care physician for a routine check-up. He was concerned about his health due to a family history of heart disease; his father had a heart attack at age 50.
John reported occasional episodes of chest tightness, particularly during stressful situations, but denied any significant pain or discomfort. He also experienced mild fatigue, especially after long workdays.
His risk factors included hyperlipidemia (total cholesterol of 240 mg/dL) and a sedentary lifestyle due to his desk job. He did not smoke, but he consumed alcohol socially and had a high-stress job in finance.
After thorough evaluations, including stress testing and imaging, it was determined that John did not have coronary artery disease. He was advised on lifestyle changes, including dietary modifications and regular exercise, to manage his cholesterol levels and reduce his risk.
