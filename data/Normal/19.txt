Sadio, A 38-year-old male presented with acute chest pain during a stressful work situation. He described the pain as sharp and located in the center of his chest, which worsened with deep breaths. The patient had a history of anxiety and panic attacks. He reported experiencing similar episodes in the past, particularly during high-stress situations. An ECG was performed, which showed normal findings. Cardiac enzymes were also within normal limits. A psychiatric evaluation indicated that his symptoms were consistent with panic disorder. The patient was referred for cognitive-behavioral therapy and prescribed an anxiolytic medication. After addressing his anxiety, he reported a significant reduction in chest pain episodes.
