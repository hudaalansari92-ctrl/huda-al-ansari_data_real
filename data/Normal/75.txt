A 55-year-old female with a history of psoriatic arthritis came in for routine follow-up. She expressed concerns about the potential impact of her condition on her cardiovascular health.
The patient-reported joint pain and occasional fatigue but denied any chest pain or cardiovascular symptoms. She was primarily concerned about the long-term effects of her chronic inflammation.
Her risk factors included chronic inflammation from psoriatic arthritis and a family history of heart disease. However, she maintained a healthy diet and exercised regularly.
After comprehensive evaluation, including imaging studies to assess her cardiovascular health, it was confirmed that she did not have coronary artery disease. The patient was advised to continue managing her arthritis effectively while being monitored for any cardiovascular changes.
