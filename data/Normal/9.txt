Ellen, a 63-year-old woman with long-standing type 2 diabetes and high cholesterol, developed sudden chest pain after gardening. The pain worsened with movement and deep breathing, making her suspect a heart attack. Given her risk factors for coronary artery disease, blood tests, including troponin and an ECG, were performed, but both were normal. An echocardiogram also showed no abnormalities. Physical examination revealed tenderness along her ribs, and Ellen was diagnosed with costochondritis, an inflammation of the cartilage in her chest. Her chest pain was not related to heart disease but was musculoskeletal in origin. Anti-inflammatory medications and rest helped resolve her symptoms.
