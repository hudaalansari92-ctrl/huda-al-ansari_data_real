A 62-year-old female with a history of psoriasis presented for evaluation due to increased fatigue and joint pain. She expressed concerns about her cardiovascular health due to her chronic inflammatory condition.
The patient reported persistent fatigue and joint swelling but denied any chest pain or palpitations. She occasionally experienced shortness of breath during flare-ups but attributed this to her arthritis rather than heart issues.
Her risk factors included chronic inflammation from psoriasis, obesity (BMI of 30), and a family history of heart disease (her father had bypass surgery).
A comprehensive evaluation showed no signs of coronary artery disease despite her chronic inflammatory condition. The patient was advised on managing her psoriasis effectively while being monitored for cardiovascular health.
