Karen, a 62-year-old woman with obesity and metabolic syndrome, presented to her physician with concerns about chest discomfort and breathlessness during mild activity. She had been diagnosed with metabolic syndrome five years earlier, which included high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and insulin resistance. Due to her risk profile, she underwent blood tests, an ECG, and an echocardiogram. Her troponin levels were normal, and her ECG showed no abnormalities. The echocardiogram demonstrated preserved heart function with no evidence of ischemia. A stress test revealed no significant coronary artery disease. Karen's symptoms were related to deconditioning and poor fitness rather than heart disease. She was advised to focus on weight loss, improve her diet, and engage in regular physical activity to lower her future risk of heart disease.
