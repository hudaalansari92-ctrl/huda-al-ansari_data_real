Tom, a 61-year-old man who had been dealing with gout for over 10 years, began to experience chest pain and discomfort during moderate physical activity. Gout, a form of arthritis caused by high uric acid levels, can lead to inflammation, and Tom was concerned that his condition may have contributed to coronary artery disease (CAD). His doctor ordered blood tests, including troponin levels, which were normal. An ECG showed no ischemic changes, and an echocardiogram confirmed normal heart function. After undergoing a stress test, Tom was found to have no evidence of CAD. His chest pain was likely caused by musculoskeletal inflammation related to gout. He was advised to continue his gout management with uric acid-lowering medication and anti-inflammatories, along with regular exercise to maintain his heart health.
