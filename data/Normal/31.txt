Pedro, A 62-year-old male with stage 3 chronic kidney disease (CKD) presented for evaluation due to elevated creatinine levels noted in routine blood work. He had multiple risk factors for cardiovascular disease, including diabetes and hypertension. He reported feeling generally well but expressed concerns about his heart health due to his CKD. A cardiac evaluation, including an echocardiogram and stress testing, was performed due to his risk factors. Both tests indicated preserved left ventricular function with no evidence of CAD. The patient was reassured that while CKD is associated with increased cardiovascular risk, he did not currently have any signs of coronary artery disease. He continued regular monitoring of his kidney function and cardiovascular health without developing CAD.
