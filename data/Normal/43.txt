Mary, a 62-year-old woman with a 20-year history of hypothyroidism, experienced occasional chest discomfort and fatigue, especially during periods of low energy. Her hypothyroidism was well-managed with levothyroxine, but given her age and symptoms, her doctor performed a full cardiac workup to rule out coronary artery disease (CAD). Blood tests, including cardiac enzymes, were normal, and her ECG showed no abnormalities. An echocardiogram revealed normal heart function, with no signs of blockages or reduced blood flow. After a stress test also showed no evidence of CAD, her chest discomfort was attributed to her hypothyroidism, as the condition can sometimes cause fatigue and mild discomfort. Mary was reassured that her heart was healthy and advised to adjust her thyroid medication slightly to better manage her symptoms.
