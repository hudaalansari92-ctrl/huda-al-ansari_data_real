A 58-year-old male diagnosed with obstructive sleep apnea presented to the clinic complaining of excessive daytime sleepiness and fatigue. He had been using CPAP therapy inconsistently due to discomfort.
The patient reported loud snoring and frequent awakenings at night. He experienced headaches in the morning and felt fatigued throughout the day but denied any chest pain or discomfort.
His risk factors included obesity (BMI of 31), a family history of heart disease, and hypertension, which was well-controlled with medication.
A thorough evaluation revealed significant obstructive sleep apnea but no coronary artery disease upon further cardiac assessment. The patient was educated on the importance of consistent CPAP use and lifestyle modifications to improve his overall health.
