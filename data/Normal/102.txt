Suha is 50 years old and suffers from sharp chest pain described as stabbing or burning, which worsens with deep breathing or changing body position. It is located on the left side of the chest and extends to the back. She also complains of shortness of breath, especially when lying down, and general fatigue and palpitations. The symptoms appear intermittently and are not related to physical exertion. The patient does not have a history of heart disease. Still, she suffers from high blood pressure that is controlled with medication, in addition to a history of recurrent anxiety attacks. Non-smoker and not obese. The initial tests showed normal results, including an electrocardiogram, stress test, and cardiac enzyme tests, with no blockage in the coronary arteries or changes on the X-ray. Based on these results, the condition was diagnosed as pericarditis, an inflammation of the membrane surrounding the heart that causes pain similar to heart disease but is characterized by its association with breathing and body posture. Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen were prescribed to relieve inflammation and pain, with a note on using corticosteroids in severe cases, along with rest and avoiding strenuous activities until symptoms improve.
