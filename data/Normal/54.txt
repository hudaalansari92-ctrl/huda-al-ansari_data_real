A 45-year-old woman presented to the clinic with chronic joint pain and fatigue, having been diagnosed with rheumatoid arthritis (RA) several years earlier. Despite adhering to her prescribed RA treatment, she continued to experience periods of inflammation. Her family history was negative for coronary artery disease (CAD), and she had no traditional risk factors such as hypertension, diabetes, or hyperlipidemia. She was a non-smoker and maintained a healthy body weight. However, given her chronic systemic inflammation from RA, her physician raised concerns about an increased risk of cardiovascular complications, particularly coronary heart disease (CHD). Further tests, including a stress test and coronary calcium scan, revealed no evidence of CAD, but she was advised to adopt an anti-inflammatory diet and increase her physical activity. The patient was also counseled on the potential long-term cardiovascular risks associated with chronic inflammation and the importance of regular monitoring, even without traditional risk factors.
