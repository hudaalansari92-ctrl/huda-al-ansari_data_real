Katty, A 60-year-old female presented with complaints of chest discomfort after gardening. She described the pain as a tightness in her chest that increased when she lifted heavy pots. The patient had no significant past medical history and was physically active. However, she had recently engaged in more strenuous gardening activities than usual. An ECG showed normal sinus rhythm without any signs of ischemia, and cardiac enzymes were within normal limits. A musculoskeletal examination indicated strain in the pectoral muscles. The patient was advised on proper lifting techniques and given a regimen of rest and ice application for muscle strain relief. She reported improvement in her symptoms within a few days.
