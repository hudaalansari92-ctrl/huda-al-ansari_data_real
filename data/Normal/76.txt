A 48-year-old male who worked in a high-stress corporate environment presented with complaints of anxiety and occasional chest tightness during stressful situations. He had no prior history of heart disease.
The patient reported experiencing tightness in his chest during periods of high stress but denied any actual pain or discomfort at rest or during physical activity.
His primary risk factors included high levels of occupational stress, a sedentary lifestyle due to long hours at work, and occasional elevated cholesterol levels. He did not smoke or consume alcohol excessively.
 After a thorough evaluation of cardiac issues, it was determined that he did not have coronary artery disease. He was referred for stress management techniques and encouraged to incorporate regular physical activity into his routine.
