Paul, a 58-year-old man with a history of hypertension, visited the emergency department after experiencing chest pain, tightness, and palpitations. The symptoms occurred mostly during stressful situations and were accompanied by shortness of breath. Given his high blood pressure and family history of heart disease, Paul feared he might be having a heart attack. Blood tests, including troponin levels, were normal, and his ECG did not show any signs of ischemia. An echocardiogram revealed normal heart function. After further evaluation, Paul was diagnosed with anxiety-related chest pain, not coronary artery disease. He was referred to a therapist and started on anti-anxiety medications, which helped control his symptoms.
