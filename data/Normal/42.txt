James, a 57-year-old man diagnosed with multiple sclerosis (MS) for over 15 years, began experiencing chest pain and fatigue during mild exertion. Concerned about his cardiovascular health, especially given the chronic nature of MS and its potential impact on overall health, he sought medical evaluation. Blood tests, including troponin levels, were normal, and his ECG showed no signs of ischemia. An echocardiogram revealed normal heart function, with an ejection fraction of 60%. A stress test also showed no evidence of coronary artery disease (CAD). James was reassured that his chest pain was not related to his heart but was likely due to muscular tension related to his MS. He was advised to continue his MS management and engage in regular, light physical activity to improve his general health.
