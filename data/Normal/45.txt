Karen, a 59-year-old woman with a long-standing history of fibromyalgia, began experiencing chest pain, fatigue, and muscle aches. Given the overlap of symptoms between fibromyalgia and potential heart problems, her doctor performed a thorough evaluation to rule out coronary artery disease (CAD). Blood tests showed normal cardiac enzyme levels, and her ECG revealed no signs of ischemia. An echocardiogram also showed normal heart function with no evidence of blockages or reduced blood flow. After a stress test came back normal, Karen's symptoms were attributed to her fibromyalgia, which can cause widespread musculoskeletal pain, including in the chest. She was reassured that her heart was healthy and her pain was managed through a combination of medications and lifestyle modifications tailored to her fibromyalgia.
