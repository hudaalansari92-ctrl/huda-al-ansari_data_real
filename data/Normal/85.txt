An 80-year-old female, referred to as Patient L, came in for evaluation due to worsening shortness of breath associated with her chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
The patient reported increased difficulty breathing during physical activities such as walking up stairs. She also noted occasional tightness in her chest but attributed it primarily to her COPD rather than cardiac issues.
Her risk factors included a long history of smoking (quit 15 years ago), advanced age, COPD, and a family history of heart disease (her brother had suffered from CAD). She also led a relatively sedentary lifestyle due to her respiratory condition.
After further evaluation, including imaging studies, it was confirmed that she did not have coronary artery disease but rather an exacerbation of COPD symptoms. She was treated with bronchodilators and advised on pulmonary rehabilitation exercises to improve her overall health.
