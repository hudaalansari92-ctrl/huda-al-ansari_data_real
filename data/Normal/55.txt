A 38-year-old woman, a high-level corporate executive, presented to her physician with complaints of frequent palpitations, insomnia, and fatigue. She had been under intense chronic stress for several years due to her demanding job and personal life challenges. Despite the absence of traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), she was a non-smoker, had a healthy body weight, normal blood pressure, and no family history of heart disease; her symptoms raised concerns about her cardiovascular health. She admitted to coping with stress through poor dietary habits and irregular exercise. A stress test and coronary angiogram were conducted, both of which showed no evidence of CAD. However, her physician warned her about the potential cardiovascular risks associated with chronic psychological stress, which can lead to endothelial dysfunction and increased heart disease risk over time. She was advised to adopt stress management techniques, such as mindfulness and regular physical activity, to mitigate the long-term effects of stress on her heart health.
