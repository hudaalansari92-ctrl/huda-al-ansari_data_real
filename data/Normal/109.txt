Laila, a 52-year-old woman, has type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, and a family history of heart disease. She began experiencing a squeezing pain in her chest that radiated to her left arm and jaw during mild physical exertion, which raised concerns that she might have coronary artery disease. She saw a cardiologist, who performed a series of tests, including an electrocardiogram (ECG), a stress test, cardiac enzyme tests, and a coronary CT angiography, but none of the tests showed any evidence of blockage in the coronary arteries. After further evaluation, she was diagnosed with Cardiac Syndrome X, a condition characterized by dysfunction of the small blood vessels in the heart, leading to decreased blood flow without any obvious blockage in the major coronary arteries. The doctor recommended a healthy lifestyle that included exercise, improved control of diabetes and blood pressure, and stress reduction, as well as prescribing medications to improve blood flow, such as beta blockers and vasodilators. After a period of treatment and follow-up, her symptoms improved and she was able to perform her daily activities with greater comfort.
