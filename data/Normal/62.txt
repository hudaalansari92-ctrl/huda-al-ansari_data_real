A 39-year-old female patient with a diagnosis of multiple sclerosis (MS) presented for a routine follow-up, reporting fatigue and occasional episodes of chest tightness. Despite her age and the absence of traditional risk factors such as hypertension, diabetes, or high cholesterol, she had a significant history of chronic inflammation due to her autoimmune condition. Over the years, she had experienced recurrent flare-ups of MS, which were associated with elevated inflammatory markers like C-reactive protein (CRP). Additionally, she reported high levels of stress related to managing her illness and its impact on her daily life. A comprehensive cardiac evaluation, including stress testing and echocardiography, revealed normal coronary arteries with no evidence of coronary artery disease (CAD).
