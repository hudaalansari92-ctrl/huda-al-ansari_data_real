A 45-year-old woman with a predominantly sedentary lifestyle visited her doctor after experiencing frequent fatigue, mild chest discomfort, and shortness of breath when performing routine tasks like grocery shopping. Although she had no traditional risk factors for coronary artery disease (CAD), she was a non-smoker, had normal cholesterol and blood pressure, and maintained a healthy weight; she reported spending most of her day seated at a desk job, with little to no regular physical activity. Concerned about her symptoms, her doctor ordered a stress test and basic cardiovascular assessments, which revealed no evidence of CAD but indicated low cardiovascular fitness and mildly elevated blood pressure during exertion. Her doctor explained that low physical activity levels could lead to reduced heart efficiency and increased stiffness in the blood vessels, which over time could raise her risk for heart disease. She was advised to gradually increase her physical activity, starting with daily walks, and to follow up regularly to monitor her cardiovascular health.
