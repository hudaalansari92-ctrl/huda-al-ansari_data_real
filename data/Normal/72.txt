A 47-year-old female named Patient V presented for a routine check-up. She had a family history of coronary heart disease; her father had a myocardial infarction at age 53, and her brother suffered a nonfatal MI at 46. Despite this family history, Patient V denied ever experiencing any cardiac symptoms.
She reported feeling well overall, with no episodes of chest pain, shortness of breath, or palpitations. She was concerned about her risk due to her family history.
Patient V was approximately 30 pounds overweight and led a sedentary lifestyle. She consumed two to three glasses of red wine daily but had never smoked.
After thorough evaluation and stress testing, it was determined that she did not have any obstructive coronary artery disease. She was advised on lifestyle modifications, including increased physical activity and dietary changes to reduce her risk factors.
