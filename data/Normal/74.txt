A 36-year-old African American female, referred to as Patient F, presented with mild hypertension controlled by an Angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor. She had a history of high blood pressure but had no previous cardiac events.
Patient F reported occasional headaches but denied any chest pain, shortness of breath, or palpitations. Her primary concern was managing her hypertension effectively.
Her risk factors included mild hypertension, a family history of heart disease, and high levels of competitiveness and stress in her personal life. However, she maintained a healthy diet and exercised regularly.
After a thorough evaluation, it was determined that she did not have coronary artery disease. The focus of her management plan was on stress reduction techniques alongside continued monitoring of her blood pressure.
