Pablo, A 55-year-old male with a history of hypertension presented for evaluation after experiencing intermittent chest pain during physical exertion. He had been diagnosed with hypertension five years prior and was on antihypertensive medication, which kept his blood pressure well-controlled at around 130/85 mmHg. Given his symptoms, he underwent a stress test and coronary angiography. The stress test showed no significant changes indicative of ischemia, and the angiography revealed normal coronary arteries. The chest pain was attributed to musculoskeletal issues rather than cardiac problems. He continued to manage his hypertension effectively with medication and lifestyle changes, remaining free from CAD.
