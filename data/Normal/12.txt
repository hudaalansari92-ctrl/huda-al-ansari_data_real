Linda, a 45-year-old woman with a history of hypertension, came to the clinic with complaints of intermittent chest pain, palpitations, and lightheadedness. She was concerned that her hypertension might have led to coronary artery disease. Blood tests, including troponin levels, were normal, and her ECG showed no significant abnormalities. An echocardiogram revealed mitral valve prolapse, a benign condition where one of the heart valves does not close properly, which can cause palpitations and chest discomfort. Despite her symptoms and hypertension, Linda's heart function was otherwise normal, and she did not have coronary artery disease. Her palpitations were managed conservatively, and she was reassured about her heart health.
