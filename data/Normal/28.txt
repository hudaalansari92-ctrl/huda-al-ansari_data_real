Aseel, A 45-year-old female presented to her primary care physician for a routine check-up. She had a body mass index (BMI) of 32, was categorized as obese, and reported a family history of heart disease. Despite her weight, she was physically active and did not smoke. She occasionally experienced shortness of breath during intense exercise but attributed it to her weight rather than cardiac issues. An ECG and echocardiogram were performed as part of her evaluation due to her obesity and family history. Both tests returned normal results, showing no signs of ischemia or structural heart disease. The patient was advised on lifestyle modifications to support weight loss but was informed that her cardiovascular health appeared stable. She continued regular exercise and dietary management without developing CAD.
