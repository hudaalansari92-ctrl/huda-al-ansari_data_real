A 28-year-old female named Sarah lived in a city with high levels of air pollution due to heavy traffic and industrial activities. She presented to her primary care physician with complaints of persistent cough, fatigue, and occasional chest tightness.
Sarah described experiencing shortness of breath during physical activity, especially on days when air quality was poor. She also reported feeling fatigued and having difficulty concentrating.
She had a history of mild asthma, which she managed with an inhaler. However, her symptoms have worsened recently, coinciding with increased local air pollution levels.
Sarah's risk factors included living in a polluted environment, being a non-smoker, and having no family history of heart disease. Her work as a teacher required her to be outdoors frequently, exposing her to higher levels of air pollutants.
After a thorough evaluation, Sarah did not have coronary artery disease. However, she was advised to limit outdoor activities on days with poor air quality and was prescribed a stronger inhaler to manage her asthma symptoms. She was also educated about the potential long-term effects of air pollution on cardiovascular health.
