Saleem, a 52-year-old man, was obese (BMI 31) and had high blood pressure, along with a family history of heart disease. He began to experience squeezing chest pain that radiated to his left arm, especially when stressed or after eating a heavy meal. He also had heartburn and frequent belching. Doctors initially thought he had coronary artery disease, and performed tests including an electrocardiogram (ECG), stress test, cardiac enzyme tests, and coronary angiography. All results were normal and showed no blockage in the coronary arteries. After further evaluation, he was diagnosed with chronic GERD with esophageal spasm, a condition that causes angina-like chest pain due to irritation of the esophagus from acid reflux. He was prescribed proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) to reduce acid secretion, and was advised to avoid fatty foods and lose weight. After adhering to treatment and lifestyle changes, his symptoms improved dramatically, and he no longer had chest pain or discomfort after eating.
