A 60-year-old female named Patient R, with a history of rheumatoid arthritis, presented for evaluation due to increased fatigue and joint pain. She expressed concerns about her cardiovascular health due to her chronic inflammatory condition.
Symptoms: The patient reported persistent fatigue, joint swelling, and episodes of chest tightness that occurred during periods of increased activity or stress. She also experienced occasional palpitations but no actual chest pain.
Risk Factors: Her risk factors included chronic inflammation from rheumatoid arthritis, obesity (BMI of 30), and a family history of cardiovascular diseases. She was also taking corticosteroids for her arthritis management.
Outcome: After comprehensive evaluations showed no signs of CAD, she was reassured about her cardiac health. The patient was educated on managing her rheumatoid arthritis effectively while being monitored for cardiovascular health.
