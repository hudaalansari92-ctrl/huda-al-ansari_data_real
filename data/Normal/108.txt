Laila, a 52-year-old woman, has no chronic diseases but lives a stressful life. She started to feel chest pain in the form of severe pressure, especially during work or emotional situations. The pain sometimes extended to the left arm and jaw, and was accompanied by heart palpitations and dizziness, which made her think she had coronary artery disease. Laila underwent several tests, including an electrocardiogram (ECG), a stress test, and a coronary angiogram, but all results showed no obvious blockage or problem in the coronary arteries. After ruling out cardiac causes, she was diagnosed with Takotsubo Cardiomyopathy, a condition caused by severe emotional or physical stress, leading to temporary weakness of the heart muscle with symptoms similar to angina or a heart attack. Laila received supportive treatment that included medications to regulate blood pressure and reduce cardiac stress, as well as guidance on practicing relaxation techniques and stress management. After a few weeks, she made a full recovery, and tests showed that her heart function had returned to normal, confirming that her condition was temporary and not related to a blockage in the coronary arteries.
