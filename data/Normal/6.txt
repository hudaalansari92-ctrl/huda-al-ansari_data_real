Susan, a 55-year-old woman, came to the emergency department complaining of sharp chest pain that worsened with deep breaths and coughing. The pain had developed suddenly and made her anxious about her heart. After undergoing blood tests, including troponin levels and an ECG, no evidence of coronary artery disease or heart attack was found. A chest X-ray and further clinical evaluation revealed pleuritis, an inflammation of the tissues surrounding the lungs. Susan's chest pain was related to lung inflammation rather than a heart condition. She was prescribed anti-inflammatory medication and advised to rest. Over the next few days, her symptoms improved, and she was reassured that her heart was healthy.
