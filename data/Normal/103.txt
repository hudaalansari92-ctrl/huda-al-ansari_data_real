Sami is 55 years old and suffers from a pressing pain in the middle of his chest that occurs at rest and is not necessarily related to physical exertion. The pain lasts for several minutes and then disappears, accompanied by a feeling of nausea and cold sweat. He also complains of difficulty swallowing and a burning sensation in the chest after eating rich meals. There is no previous history of heart disease, but the patient suffers from obesity (body mass index 32) and high cholesterol levels, with a sedentary lifestyle and lack of regular exercise. The initial tests showed normal results, including an electrocardiogram, stress test, and cardiac enzymes, and the coronary angiography did not reveal any blockage in the coronary arteries. Based on that, the patient was diagnosed with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), which can cause chest pain similar to angina, especially after eating fatty meals or when lying down. Antacids such as proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) were prescribed to reduce acid secretion, along with dietary advice to avoid fatty and spicy foods, and not to lie down after eating. Additionally, the patient was encouraged to lose weight and increase physical activity to improve symptoms, with regular follow-ups with a gastroenterologist to rule out any potential complications such as esophagitis.
