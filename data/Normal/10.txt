Tom, a 55-year-old former smoker with a history of hypertension, experienced sudden episodes of chest tightness, racing heart, and dizziness while driving. Convinced that these were heart-related, he sought medical care. Blood tests, including troponin levels, were normal, and his ECG showed no signs of heart disease. A stress test also returned normal results. After further evaluation, Tom was diagnosed with panic disorder. His chest pain and other symptoms were caused by panic attacks rather than coronary artery disease. He was referred to a psychologist for cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), and with appropriate treatment, his symptoms gradually improved.
