George, a 61-year-old man with a history of high cholesterol, developed chest pain after lifting heavy boxes during a home renovation. The pain was sharp and localized, leading him to believe it could be heart-related due to his cholesterol levels. Blood tests and an ECG revealed no cardiac abnormalities, and his echocardiogram showed normal heart function. Upon examination, his pain was reproducible with movement and pressure on his chest wall, leading to a diagnosis of muscle strain. His chest pain was musculoskeletal, not cardiac, in nature. He was advised to rest and use anti-inflammatory medications, which resolved his symptoms over time.
