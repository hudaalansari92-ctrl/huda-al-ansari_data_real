David, a 60-year-old man with a 15-year history of type 2 diabetes, presented to his doctor with concerns about chest pain and fatigue during physical exertion. Given his long-standing diabetes, poorly controlled with an HbA1c of 8.5%, he was at high risk for coronary artery disease (CAD). He had other risk factors, including obesity and a family history of heart disease. Blood tests, including troponin levels, were normal, and his ECG showed no signs of ischemia. A stress test was performed, which showed no evidence of significant blockages, and his echocardiogram revealed normal heart function with an ejection fraction of 55%. Despite his high risk due to diabetes, David was not found to have CAD. His chest pain was attributed to musculoskeletal strain, and he was advised to better manage his diabetes through lifestyle changes and medication to reduce his future risk of developing heart disease.
