Gazi, A 42-year-old male sought medical attention for recurrent episodes of chest pain that felt similar to angina, particularly after eating spicy foods. He had no history of heart disease but was concerned about his symptoms. After ruling out cardiac causes through an ECG and cardiac enzyme testing, an esophageal motility study was conducted, which revealed intermittent esophageal spasms. The patient was educated about dietary modifications and prescribed medications to help manage esophageal spasms. His symptoms improved significantly after implementing these changes.
