A 48-year-old woman who worked at a factory located near an industrial zone with high particulate matter (PM2.5) levels visited her doctor due to persistent fatigue, mild chest tightness, and shortness of breath that worsened after work shifts. She had no family history of coronary artery disease and led a generally healthy lifestyle. Her physician suspected that chronic exposure to industrial pollutants might lead to oxidative stress and systemic inflammation, a precursor to endothelial dysfunction and early atherosclerosis. Although she had no signs of CAD on initial testing, she was advised to wear a protective mask at work, consider indoor air purification at home, and incorporate antioxidant-rich foods into her diet to help combat the inflammatory effects of pollution.
