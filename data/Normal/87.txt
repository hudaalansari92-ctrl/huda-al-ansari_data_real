A 52-year-old man who worked as a traffic controller at a busy airport visited his doctor complaining of occasional chest tightness and fatigue, especially after long shifts. He had no traditional risk factors for CAD; he was a nonsmoker, had normal cholesterol levels, and maintained a healthy weight. His physician noted that his chronic exposure to high levels of particulate matter (PM2.5) from jet fuel and vehicle exhaust could be causing oxidative stress and low-grade inflammation, which may lead to endothelial dysfunction and increase his risk for future atherosclerosis. He was advised to limit exposure by wearing a mask at work, taking regular breaks in areas with cleaner air, and considering antioxidant supplements to support his vascular health.
