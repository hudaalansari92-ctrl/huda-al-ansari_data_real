A 70-year-old male named Robert came in for evaluation of his chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). He expressed concerns about his heart health due to his breathing difficulties.
Robert reported shortness of breath during physical activity and occasional chest tightness when exerting himself. He denied any episodes of angina or significant pain.
His risk factors included a long history of smoking (he quit 10 years ago), COPD, and high levels of work-related stress as a construction manager. He also had hypertension, but it was well-controlled with medication.
After further evaluation, including pulmonary function tests and cardiac assessments, Robert was found not to have coronary artery disease. His symptoms were attributed primarily to COPD, and he was advised on managing both conditions effectively.
