A 45-year-old woman visited her primary care physician with complaints of frequent fatigue, mild chest discomfort, and shortness of breath during her commute through a heavily polluted urban area. She reported that her symptoms worsened on high-pollution days, especially during rush hour when air quality was poor. Her doctor explained that chronic exposure to particulate matter (PM2.5) may trigger oxidative stress and systemic inflammation, leading to early endothelial dysfunction, a precursor to atherosclerosis. Though her cardiac tests showed no evidence of CAD, she was advised to avoid peak traffic times, use air filtration in her home, and adopt a diet rich in antioxidants to counteract oxidative stress and help protect her vascular health.
